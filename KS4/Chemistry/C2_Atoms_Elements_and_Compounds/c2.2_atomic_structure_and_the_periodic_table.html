C2.2 Atomic structure and the Periodic Table

The atom is the fundamental building block of all matter. Understanding its structure, from the central nucleus to the orbiting electrons, is essential for comprehending how elements behave and how they are organized in the Periodic Table. This section delves into the subatomic particles, atomic notation, and electron arrangement.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the structure of the atom as a central nucleus containing neutrons and protons, surrounded by electrons in shells.
  2. State the relative charges and relative masses of a proton, a neutron and an electron.
  3. Determine the electronic configuration of elements with proton number 1 to 20, e.g. 2,8,3.
  4. State that: Group VIII noble gases have a full outer shell; the number of outer-shell electrons is equal to the group number in Groups I to VII; the number of occupied electron shells is equal to the period number.

Atomic Structure:

An atom consists of a tiny, dense **central nucleus** surrounded by electrons orbiting in specific energy levels or **shells**.

The nucleus contains two types of subatomic particles:

  • **Protons:** Positively charged particles. Relative charge = +1. Relative mass = 1.
  • **Neutrons:** Neutral particles (no charge). Relative charge = 0. Relative mass = 1.

**Electrons** are negatively charged particles that orbit the nucleus in electron shells. Relative charge = -1. Relative mass is negligible (approximately 1/1836 of a proton).

*Figure: A simplified diagram of an atom, showing the central nucleus with protons and neutrons, and electrons orbiting in shells.*

Electronic Configuration and the Periodic Table:

The **electronic configuration** describes how electrons are arranged in shells around the nucleus. For elements with proton number 1 to 20, electrons fill shells according to these rules:

  • 1st shell: maximum 2 electrons
  • 2nd shell: maximum 8 electrons
  • 3rd shell: maximum 8 electrons (for elements up to proton number 20)

The electronic configuration is directly related to an element's position in the **Periodic Table**:

  • **Group Number (I to VII):** The number of outer-shell electrons is equal to the group number. For example, Group I elements have 1 outer electron.
  • **Group VIII (Noble Gases):** These elements have a full outer shell of electrons (2 for Helium, 8 for others), making them very stable and unreactive.
  • **Period Number:** The number of occupied electron shells is equal to the period number. For example, elements in Period 2 have 2 occupied electron shells.
