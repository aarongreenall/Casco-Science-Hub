B14.1 Drugs

**Drugs** are substances that, when introduced into the body, alter its normal chemical reactions. They can have a wide range of effects, from relieving pain and treating diseases to altering mood and behavior. Understanding the categories of drugs, their effects, and the risks associated with misuse is crucial for public health.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Define a drug as any substance taken into the body that modifies or affects chemical reactions in the body.
  2. Describe the effects of: heroin, limited to: a depressant; highly addictive; causes withdrawal symptoms; causes crime; causes spread of HIV/AIDS.
  3. Describe the effects of: alcohol, limited to: a depressant; causes slower reaction times; causes addiction; causes liver damage; causes brain damage; causes social harms.
  4. Describe the effects of: tobacco, limited to: nicotine – addictive; tar – causes cancer; carbon monoxide – reduces oxygen transport by red blood cells.
  5. State that antibiotics kill bacteria but not viruses.
  6. State that antibiotics are used to treat bacterial infections.
  7. State that antibiotics are not effective against viruses.
  8. State that the overuse of antibiotics can lead to the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

What are Drugs?

A **drug** is any substance taken into the body that modifies or affects chemical reactions in the body. Drugs can be beneficial (medicines) or harmful (substances of abuse).

*Figure: Various types of medications, which are a common form of drugs.*

Specific Drugs and Their Effects:

1. Heroin:

Heroin is an illegal drug with severe effects:

  • **Depressant:** Slows down brain activity, leading to reduced pain perception and feelings of euphoria.
  • **Highly Addictive:** Causes strong physical and psychological dependence.
  • **Withdrawal Symptoms:** Severe and painful symptoms occur if use is stopped.
  • **Causes Crime:** Addiction often leads to criminal activity to fund the drug habit.
  • **Causes Spread of HIV/AIDS:** Sharing contaminated needles for injection is a major route of transmission for HIV.

2. Alcohol (Ethanol):

Alcohol is a legal drug (for adults) but can have significant harmful effects:

  • **Depressant:** Slows down the nervous system, impairing judgment, coordination, and reaction times.
  • **Causes Addiction:** Can lead to physical and psychological dependence.
  • **Liver Damage:** Chronic heavy drinking can lead to liver cirrhosis (scarring of the liver), which is irreversible and life-threatening.
  • **Brain Damage:** Long-term misuse can cause irreversible brain damage.
  • **Social Harms:** Contributes to accidents, violence, and various social problems.

3. Tobacco (Smoking):

Tobacco smoke contains several harmful substances:

  • **Nicotine:** The addictive component, causing physical dependence and increasing heart rate and blood pressure.
  • **Tar:** A sticky, black substance that accumulates in the lungs, paralyzes cilia, causes smoker's cough, and is carcinogenic (causes cancer, especially lung cancer).
  • **Carbon Monoxide (CO):** A toxic gas that binds irreversibly to haemoglobin in red blood cells, reducing their oxygen-carrying capacity. This puts strain on the heart and can lead to oxygen deprivation in tissues.

*Figure: Comparison of a healthy lung (left) and a smoker's lung (right), showing the damage caused by smoking.*

Antibiotics:

**Antibiotics** are a class of drugs used to treat **bacterial infections**. They work by killing or inhibiting the growth of bacteria.

**Important points about antibiotics:**

  • Antibiotics **kill bacteria but not viruses**. This means they are ineffective against viral infections like the common cold or flu.
  • **Overuse of antibiotics** can lead to the development of **antibiotic-resistant bacteria**. This occurs when bacteria evolve to become resistant to the effects of antibiotics, making infections much harder to treat. This is a major global health concern.
