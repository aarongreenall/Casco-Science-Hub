B15.5 Sexually transmitted infections

**Sexually transmitted infections (STIs)**, also known as sexually transmitted diseases (STDs), are infections that are primarily spread through sexual contact. They are a significant public health concern globally. Understanding how STIs are transmitted, their effects, and methods of prevention is crucial for promoting sexual health and preventing their spread.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the causes and effects of HIV/AIDS, limited to: HIV as the virus that causes AIDS; HIV is transmitted by the exchange of body fluids; HIV affects the immune system, increasing the risk of opportunistic infections; AIDS is the final stage of HIV infection.
  2. Describe the methods of transmission of HIV.
  3. Describe the methods of preventing the spread of HIV.

HIV/AIDS: Causes, Effects, and Transmission:

**HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)** is a virus that attacks the body's immune system. Over time, if left untreated, HIV can lead to **AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome)**, which is the final and most severe stage of HIV infection.

*Figure: Diagram of the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV).*

Causes and Effects of HIV/AIDS:

  • **Cause:** HIV is the virus that specifically targets and destroys white blood cells called **T-helper lymphocytes**, which are crucial components of the immune system.
  • **Effects:** As HIV destroys these immune cells, the immune system becomes progressively weaker. This increases the individual's susceptibility to **opportunistic infections** (infections that a healthy immune system could easily fight off, but which become severe or life-threatening in an immunocompromised person). AIDS is diagnosed when the immune system is severely compromised and opportunistic infections or certain cancers develop.

Transmission of HIV:

HIV is transmitted through the **exchange of body fluids** that contain the virus. The main methods of transmission are:

  • **Unprotected sexual intercourse:** Vaginal, anal, or oral sex without a condom.
  • **Sharing contaminated needles:** Used for injecting drugs.
  • **Mother to child transmission:** During pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding.
  • **Blood transfusions:** (Rare in countries with strict screening).

HIV is **not** transmitted through casual contact like hugging, sharing food, or insect bites.

Preventing the Spread of HIV:

Preventing the spread of HIV relies on avoiding the exchange of infected body fluids:

  • **Safe sexual practices:** Consistent and correct use of condoms during sexual intercourse.
  • **Avoiding sharing needles:** For drug injection.
  • **Screening blood products:** Ensuring blood used for transfusions is tested for HIV.
  • **Antiretroviral therapy (ART) for pregnant mothers:** Can significantly reduce the risk of mother-to-child transmission.
  • **Education and awareness:** Promoting understanding of HIV transmission and prevention.
