9.5.1 Excretory (Renal) System

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will describe the function of the excretory system in removing waste products from the body.

What is Excretion?

  • The removal from the body of waste products of metabolism, toxic materials, and substances in excess of requirements.
  • Not to be confused with egestion (the removal of undigested food as faeces).

The Renal System:

  • The main organs of the excretory system.
  • Kidneys: Two bean-shaped organs that filter waste products from the blood to produce urine.
  • Ureters: Tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder.
  • Bladder: A muscular sac that stores urine.
  • Urethra: The tube through which urine is expelled from the body.

How the Kidneys Work:

  • The kidneys filter the blood, removing a toxic waste product called urea, which is produced in the liver from excess amino acids.
  • They also regulate the water balance of the body, a process called osmoregulation. They remove excess water and mineral ions to keep the blood concentration constant.
  • The filtered waste products, excess water, and excess ions form urine.
