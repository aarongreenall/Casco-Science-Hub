B9.1 Circulatory systems

Multicellular organisms, especially larger animals, require specialized **circulatory systems** to transport essential substances like oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout their bodies. These systems overcome the limitations of diffusion over long distances, ensuring that all cells receive what they need and waste is efficiently removed.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the need for a transport system in multicellular organisms.
  2. Describe the circulatory system of a mammal as a closed, double circulatory system.
  3. Describe the advantages of a double circulatory system.
  4. State that the heart is a muscular pump that forces blood around the body.

Need for Transport Systems:

In single-celled organisms, substances like oxygen and nutrients can move directly into and out of the cell by diffusion because the diffusion distance is very short. However, in **multicellular organisms**, especially larger ones, cells are far from the external environment. Diffusion alone is too slow and inefficient to transport substances to all cells in the body. Therefore, specialized **transport systems** are needed to:

  • Deliver oxygen and nutrients to all cells.
  • Remove metabolic waste products (like carbon dioxide and urea) from cells.
  • Transport hormones and other signaling molecules.

*Figure: A diagram of the human circulatory system, showing the heart, lungs, and body circulation.*

Mammalian Circulatory System: Closed and Double:

Mammals, including humans, have a **closed, double circulatory system**:

  • **Closed System:** Blood is always contained within blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries) and does not directly bathe the body tissues. This allows for higher blood pressure and more efficient transport.
  • **Double Circulatory System:** Blood passes through the heart twice for each complete circuit of the body. It consists of two separate loops: * **Pulmonary Circulation:** Blood is pumped from the heart to the lungs (to pick up oxygen and release carbon dioxide) and then returns to the heart. * **Systemic Circulation:** Blood is pumped from the heart to the rest of the body (to deliver oxygen and nutrients) and then returns to the heart.

*Figure: Diagram illustrating a double circulatory system, showing separate pulmonary and systemic circuits.*

Advantages of a Double Circulatory System:

The double circulatory system offers significant advantages for efficient transport:

  • **Higher Pressure to Body Tissues:** After blood leaves the lungs, it returns to the heart to be pumped out again to the rest of the body. This allows blood to be pumped to the body tissues at a higher pressure, ensuring a faster and more efficient delivery of oxygen and nutrients.
  • **Separation of Oxygenated and Deoxygenated Blood:** The two circuits keep oxygenated blood (from the lungs) completely separate from deoxygenated blood (from the body), maximizing oxygen delivery efficiency.

The Heart: A Muscular Pump:

The **heart** is a vital organ that acts as a muscular pump, forcing blood around the body through the circulatory system. Its powerful contractions ensure that blood reaches all tissues and organs, maintaining the continuous flow necessary for life.
