B7.1 Diet

A **balanced diet** is essential for maintaining good health, growth, and development. It provides the body with all the necessary nutrients in the correct proportions. This section explores what constitutes a balanced diet, the principal dietary sources and importance of various nutrients, and the consequences of their deficiencies.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe what is meant by a balanced diet.
  2. State the principal dietary sources and describe the importance of: carbohydrates; fats and oils; proteins; vitamins, limited to C and D; mineral ions, limited to calcium and iron; fibre (roughage); water.
  3. State the causes of scurvy and rickets.

What is a Balanced Diet?

A **balanced diet** is one that provides all the essential nutrients in the correct proportions to meet the body's needs for energy, growth, repair, and overall health. It ensures that the body receives enough energy and all the necessary macronutrients (carbohydrates, fats, proteins) and micronutrients (vitamins, minerals, water, fibre).

*Figure: A visual guide to a balanced diet, often represented by a healthy eating plate.*

Principal Nutrients: Sources and Importance:

Each type of nutrient plays a specific role in the body:

  • **Carbohydrates:** * **Sources:** Bread, pasta, rice, potatoes, fruits, sugary foods. * **Importance:** Primary source of energy for the body.
  • **Fats and Oils (Lipids):** * **Sources:** Butter, oils, nuts, fatty meats, dairy products. * **Importance:** Long-term energy storage, insulation, protection of organs, essential for cell membranes, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.
  • **Proteins:** * **Sources:** Meat, fish, eggs, dairy, beans, lentils, nuts. * **Importance:** Essential for growth and repair of tissues, making enzymes, hormones, and antibodies.
  • **Vitamins (limited to C and D):** * **Vitamin C:** * **Sources:** Citrus fruits (oranges, lemons), berries, leafy green vegetables. * **Importance:** Essential for healthy skin, gums, and blood vessels; acts as an antioxidant. * **Vitamin D:** * **Sources:** Oily fish, egg yolks, fortified foods; synthesized in skin exposed to sunlight. * **Importance:** Essential for the absorption of calcium and phosphorus, crucial for healthy bones and teeth.
  • **Mineral Ions (limited to Calcium and Iron):** * **Calcium (Ca$^{2+}$):** * **Sources:** Dairy products (milk, cheese), leafy green vegetables. * **Importance:** Essential for strong bones and teeth, blood clotting, and muscle contraction. * **Iron (Fe$^{2+}$):** * **Sources:** Red meat, liver, leafy green vegetables, fortified cereals. * **Importance:** Essential for making haemoglobin in red blood cells, which transports oxygen.
  • **Fibre (Roughage):** * **Sources:** Whole grains, fruits, vegetables. * **Importance:** Provides bulk to food, aiding peristalsis (movement of food through the digestive tract) and preventing constipation.
  • **Water:** * **Sources:** Drinking water, beverages, fruits, vegetables. * **Importance:** Essential for all bodily functions; acts as a solvent, transports substances, regulates body temperature, and is involved in chemical reactions.

Deficiency Diseases:

Lack of specific nutrients in the diet can lead to deficiency diseases:

  • **Scurvy:** Caused by a deficiency of **Vitamin C**. Symptoms include bleeding gums, bruising, weakness, and slow wound healing.
  • **Rickets:** Caused by a deficiency of **Vitamin D** (and/or calcium). Symptoms include soft, weak bones, leading to bone deformities (e.g., bowed legs) in children.
