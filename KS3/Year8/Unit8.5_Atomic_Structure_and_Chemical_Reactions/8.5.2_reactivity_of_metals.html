8.5.2 Reactivity of Metals

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will introduce the concept of the reactivity series of metals and how it is determined.

Reactivity Series:

  • An arrangement of metals in order of their reactivity, from most reactive to least reactive.
  • A more reactive metal can displace a less reactive metal from its compound.
  • The reactivity of a metal is related to its tendency to lose electrons and form positive ions.

Determining Reactivity:

A simple reactivity series can be determined by observing the reactions of metals with:

  • Water: Very reactive metals (like potassium and sodium) react vigorously with cold water. Less reactive metals (like magnesium and zinc) react with steam. Unreactive metals (like copper and gold) do not react.
  • Acid (e.g., dilute hydrochloric acid): Metals above hydrogen in the reactivity series will react with dilute acids to produce a salt and hydrogen gas. The speed of the reaction (rate of bubbling) indicates reactivity. Metals below hydrogen will not react.
