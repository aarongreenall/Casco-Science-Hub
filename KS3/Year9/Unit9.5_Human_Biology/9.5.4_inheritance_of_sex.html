9.5.4 Inheritance of Sex

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how sex is determined in humans by the sex chromosomes.

Sex Chromosomes:

  • Of the 23 pairs of chromosomes in humans, one pair is the sex chromosomes.
  • These chromosomes determine the biological sex of an individual.
  • There are two types of sex chromosomes: X and Y.
  • Females have two X chromosomes (XX).
  • Males have one X and one Y chromosome (XY).

Inheritance:

  • Since females are XX, all their egg cells (gametes) contain a single X chromosome.
  • Since males are XY, half of their sperm cells (gametes) contain an X chromosome, and the other half contain a Y chromosome.
  • The sex of the offspring is determined by which type of sperm fertilizes the egg.
    • If an X sperm fertilizes the egg (X), the resulting zygote is XX (female).
    • If a Y sperm fertilizes the egg (X), the resulting zygote is XY (male).

Punnett Square:

A Punnett square can be used to show that there is a 50% probability of having a male child and a 50% probability of having a female child at each conception.

Sperm (from Father) Sperm (from Father)
X Y
Egg (from Mother) X XX XY
Egg (from Mother) X XX XY

Result: 2 XX (female) and 2 XY (male) = 50% chance of each.
