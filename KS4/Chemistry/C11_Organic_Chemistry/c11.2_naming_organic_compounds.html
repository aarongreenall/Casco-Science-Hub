C11.2 Naming organic compounds

With the vast number of organic compounds, a systematic method for naming them is essential. This section introduces the basic principles of naming simple organic compounds based on their homologous series and the number of carbon atoms in their longest chain.

Learning Objectives:

  1. State the type of compound present given a chemical name ending in -ane, -ene or -ol, or from a molecular formula or displayed formula.
  2. Name and draw the displayed formulas of: methane and ethane.
  3. Name and draw the structural formulas and displayed formulas of unbranched: alkanes, containing up to four carbon atoms per molecule; alkenes, including but-1-ene and but-2-ene (not cis/trans), containing up to four carbon atoms per molecule; ethanol.

Systematic Naming (IUPAC Nomenclature):

Organic compounds are named systematically using rules established by the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry (IUPAC). The name of an organic compound typically indicates:

  • **Prefix:** Indicates the number of carbon atoms in the longest continuous chain. * 1 carbon: `meth-` * 2 carbons: `eth-` * 3 carbons: `prop-` * 4 carbons: `but-`
  • **Suffix:** Indicates the homologous series (type of compound) and the type of bonding present. * `-ane`: Alkanes (all single C-C bonds) * `-ene`: Alkenes (at least one C=C double bond) * `-ol`: Alcohols (contain an -OH functional group)

By combining the prefix and suffix, you can deduce the type of compound from its chemical name. You should also be able to do this from its molecular formula or displayed formula.

*Figure: Common prefixes and suffixes used in naming simple organic compounds.*

Naming and Drawing Alkanes:

**Alkanes** are saturated hydrocarbons (contain only carbon and hydrogen, with all carbon-carbon single bonds) with the general formula C$_n$H$_{2n+2}$. For unbranched alkanes containing up to four carbon atoms:

  • **Methane (CH$_4$):** 1 carbon atom.
  • **Ethane (C$_2$H$_6$):** 2 carbon atoms.
  • **Propane (C$_3$H$_8$):** 3 carbon atoms.
  • **Butane (C$_4$H$_{10}$):** 4 carbon atoms.

You should be able to draw their **displayed formulas**, showing all atoms and all single bonds.

*Figure: Displayed formulas for the first four alkanes: methane, ethane, propane, and butane.*

Naming and Drawing Alkenes:

**Alkenes** are unsaturated hydrocarbons (contain at least one carbon-carbon double bond) with the general formula C$_n$H$_{2n}$. For unbranched alkenes containing up to four carbon atoms:

  • **Ethene (C$_2$H$_4$):** 2 carbon atoms, with a C=C double bond.
  • **Propene (C$_3$H$_6$):** 3 carbon atoms, with a C=C double bond.
  • **But-1-ene (C$_4$H$_8$):** 4 carbon atoms, with the C=C double bond between the first and second carbon atoms.
  • **But-2-ene (C$_4$H$_8$):** 4 carbon atoms, with the C=C double bond between the second and third carbon atoms. (You do not need to distinguish between cis/trans isomers).

*Figure: Displayed formulas for ethene, propene, but-1-ene, and but-2-ene.*

Naming and Drawing Alcohols:

**Alcohols** are organic compounds containing the hydroxyl (-OH) functional group. The simplest alcohol you need to know is ethanol.

  • **Ethanol (C$_2$H$_5$OH):** 2 carbon atoms, with an -OH group.

*Figure: The displayed formula of ethanol.*
