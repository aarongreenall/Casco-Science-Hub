7.7.1 Plate tectonics model

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Describe the structure of the Earth in terms of crust, mantle, and core.
  2. Define a tectonic plate.
  3. Explain how convection currents in the mantle cause the tectonic plates to move.

The Structure of the Earth

Our planet is not a solid ball of rock. It is made up of several distinct layers, a bit like an onion. The three main layers are:

  • Crust: A very thin, rocky outer layer. This is the part we live on.
  • Mantle: A very thick layer beneath the crust. It is mostly solid rock, but it is so hot that it can flow very slowly, like thick treacle.
  • Core: The centre of the Earth, made of iron and nickel. It has a solid inner core and a liquid outer core. The heat from the core is what drives the movement of the mantle.

Tectonic Plates and Convection Currents

The Earth's crust is not one solid piece. It is broken up into several huge, rigid slabs called **tectonic plates**. These plates "float" on top of the slowly moving mantle.

The movement of these plates is driven by heat from the Earth's core. This heat creates very slow **convection currents** within the mantle.

  1. Hot, less dense rock in the mantle slowly rises.
  2. When it reaches the top, it spreads out, dragging the tectonic plates along with it.
  3. As the rock cools, it becomes denser and sinks back down towards the core.

This continuous, slow-moving cycle is what causes the continents to drift, mountains to form, and earthquakes and volcanoes to occur at the plate boundaries.

In-School Activity: Mapping the Evidence

Your teacher will give you two maps: one showing the Earth's tectonic plates (like the one above) and another showing the locations of the world's major earthquakes and volcanoes.

Your Task:
  1. Place a sheet of tracing paper over the earthquake and volcano map and mark the locations of these events.
  2. Now, place the tracing paper over the map of the tectonic plates.
  3. What do you notice about the relationship between the locations of earthquakes and volcanoes and the boundaries of the tectonic plates? Write a short sentence to describe the pattern you see.
