7.4.3 Dichotomous keys

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define a dichotomous key as a tool for identifying organisms.
  2. Explain how to use a dichotomous key by following a series of paired choices.
  3. Apply a dichotomous key to identify different organisms.

What is a Dichotomous Key?

Imagine you're a biologist who has found a new insect you've never seen before. How would you figure out what it is? Scientists use a special tool called a **dichotomous key** to identify unknown organisms.

The word "dichotomous" means "divided into two parts." A dichotomous key is a series of questions, where each question has only two possible answers (like "yes" or "no," or "has wings" / "does not have wings"). By answering each question, you are led to the next question, which narrows down the possibilities until you have identified your organism.

Analogy: The "Guess Who?" Game

A dichotomous key works exactly like the board game "Guess Who?". In the game, you ask a series of yes/no questions to eliminate characters. "Does your person have glasses?" If the answer is yes, you flip down all the characters without glasses. You continue asking questions until only one character is left. A dichotomous key does the same thing, but for identifying real living things.

How to Use a Dichotomous Key

Let's use a key to identify the animals in the image below. We'll try to identify the **Spider**.

Example Key:

  • 1a. Does the animal have wings? .................... Go to 2
  • 1b. Does the animal not have wings? .............. Go to 3

  • 2a. Are the wings covered in feathers? ............ Bird
  • 2b. Are the wings covered in scales? ............. Butterfly

  • 3a. Does the animal have legs? .................... Spider
  • 3b. Does the animal not have legs? ................ Go to 4

  • 4a. Does it have scales and live on land? ......... Snake
  • 4b. Does it have scales and live in water? ....... Fish

To identify the Spider, we follow the steps:

  1. **Start at 1:** Does the spider have wings? No. So we follow instruction **1b** and go to step 3.
  2. **Go to 3:** Does the spider have legs? Yes. So we follow instruction **3a**.
  3. **Result:** The key tells us the animal is a **Spider**. We have successfully identified it!
