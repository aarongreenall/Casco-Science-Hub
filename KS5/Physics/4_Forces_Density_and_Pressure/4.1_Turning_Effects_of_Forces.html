4.1 Turning Effects of Forces

Learning Objectives:

  1. Understand that the weight of an object may be taken as acting at a single point known as its centre of gravity.
  2. Define and apply the moment of a force.
  3. Understand that a couple is a pair of forces that acts to produce rotation only.
  4. Define and apply the torque of a couple.

LO1: Centre of Gravity

The **centre of gravity (CoG)** is the single imaginary point through which the entire weight of the object can be considered to act. This allows us to simplify problems by treating the distributed weight of an object as a single force acting at one point.

LO2: Moment of a Force

A **moment** is the turning effect of a force about a specific point, known as the **pivot** or **fulcrum**.

The moment of a force is defined as the product of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force.

$$ \text{Moment} = F \times d_{\perp} $$

Worked Example 1: Balancing a See-saw

A child of mass 30 kg sits 2.0 m from the pivot of a see-saw. Where must a child of mass 40 kg sit to balance the see-saw? (Take $g = 9.81$ N kg⁻¹).

1. State the Principle of Moments: For equilibrium, Clockwise Moments = Anticlockwise Moments.

2. Calculate the anticlockwise moment: The 30 kg child creates an anticlockwise moment.

$$ M_{ACW} = F_1 \times d_1 = (m_1g) \times d_1 = (30 \times 9.81) \times 2.0 = 588.6 \text{ N m} $$

3. Set up the clockwise moment equation: The 40 kg child must create an equal clockwise moment at distance $d_2$.

$$ M_{CW} = F_2 \times d_2 = (m_2g) \times d_2 = (40 \times 9.81) \times d_2 = 392.4 \times d_2 $$

4. Equate the moments and solve for $d_2$:

$$ 588.6 = 392.4 \times d_2 \implies d_2 = \frac{588.6}{392.4} = 1.5 \text{ m} $$

Worked Example 2: Forces on the Same Side (Balancing)

A light rod is pivoted at its left end. A downward weight of 40 N acts at a distance of 0.80 m from the pivot. What upward vertical force, F, must be applied at a distance of 0.50 m from the pivot to keep the rod in rotational equilibrium?

1. Identify the moments about the pivot:

The 40 N weight creates a clockwise moment: $M_{CW} = 40 \text{ N} \times 0.80 \text{ m} = 32 \text{ N m}$.

The upward force F creates an anticlockwise moment: $M_{ACW} = F \times 0.50 \text{ m}$.

2. Apply the Principle of Moments for equilibrium:

$$ M_{ACW} = M_{CW} $$

$$ F \times 0.50 = 32 $$

3. Solve for F:

$$ F = \frac{32}{0.50} = 64 \text{ N} $$

LO3 & LO4: Couples and Torque

A **couple** is a pair of forces that are equal in magnitude, parallel, but opposite in direction. A couple produces a **pure turning effect** without causing any linear acceleration because the resultant force is zero. The turning effect of a couple is called its **torque ($\tau$)**.

The torque of a couple is the product of one of the forces and the perpendicular distance between the lines of action of the two forces.

$$ \tau = F \times d $$

Worked Example 3: Torque of a Couple

A driver turns a steering wheel of diameter 40 cm by applying equal and opposite forces of 15 N at the rim. Calculate the torque applied to the wheel.

1. Identify the variables:

The force, $F = 15$ N. The perpendicular distance between the forces, $d$, is the diameter of the wheel.

2. Convert to SI units: $d = 40 \text{ cm} = 0.40 \text{ m}$

3. State the formula and solve:

$$ \tau = F \times d = 15 \text{ N} \times 0.40 \text{ m} = 6.0 \text{ N m} $$
