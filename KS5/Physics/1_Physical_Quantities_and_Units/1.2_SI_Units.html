1.2 SI Units

Learning Objectives:

  1. Recall the following SI base quantities and their units: mass (kg), length (m), time (s), current (A), temperature (K).
  2. Express derived units as products or quotients of the SI base units.
  3. Use SI base units to check the homogeneity of physical equations.
  4. Recall and use prefixes and their symbols.

Derived Units & Homogeneity

**Derived units** are combinations of base units. A valid equation must be **dimensionally homogeneous**, meaning the base units on both sides are identical. This is a powerful tool for checking the validity of an equation.

Worked Example: Homogeneity

Show that the equation for the pressure exerted by a fluid, $p = h \rho g$, is dimensionally homogeneous.

1. Analyse the Left-Hand Side (LHS):

The unit of pressure ($p$) is the pascal (Pa). A pascal is defined as one newton per square metre ($ \text{N m}^{-2} $). The newton (N) is a derived unit for force, which in base units is $ \text{kg m s}^{-2} $. Therefore:

$$ \text{Units of } p = \text{Pa} = \text{N m}^{-2} = (\text{kg m s}^{-2}) \times \text{m}^{-2} = \text{kg m}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2} $$

2. Analyse the Right-Hand Side (RHS):

The RHS consists of height ($h$), density ($\rho$), and gravitational field strength ($g$). Their base units are:

  • Unit of $h$ = m
  • Unit of $\rho$ = kg m⁻³
  • Unit of $g$ = m s⁻²

Combining these:

$$ \text{Units of } h \rho g = \text{m} \times (\text{kg m}^{-3}) \times (\text{m s}^{-2}) $$

$$ = \text{kg} \times (\text{m} \times \text{m}^{-3} \times \text{m}) \times \text{s}^{-2} $$

$$ = \text{kg} \times \text{m}^{(1-3+1)} \times \text{s}^{-2} = \text{kg m}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2} $$

3. Conclusion:

The base units of the LHS ($ \text{kg m}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2} $) are identical to the base units of the RHS ($ \text{kg m}^{-1} \text{s}^{-2} $). Therefore, the equation is dimensionally homogeneous.
