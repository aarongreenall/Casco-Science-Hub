8.2.2 Purity and Concentration of Solutions

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain the concepts of purity and concentration in the context of solutions.

Purity:

  • A pure liquid (like pure water) consists of only one type of molecule.
  • Pure liquids have a fixed, sharp boiling point.
  • Impurities (dissolved solids) will raise the boiling point and cause the liquid to boil over a range of temperatures.

Solutions and Concentration:

  • Solution: A mixture formed when a solute dissolves in a solvent.
    • Solute: The substance that dissolves (e.g., salt).
    • Solvent: The liquid that does the dissolving (e.g., water).
  • Concentration: A measure of the amount of solute dissolved in a certain amount of solvent.
    • Concentrated Solution: Has a large amount of solute in a small amount of solvent.
    • Dilute Solution: Has a small amount of solute in a large amount of solvent.
  • Concentration can be described qualitatively (e.g., "strong" vs "weak" juice) or quantitatively (e.g., grams per dmÂ³).
