7.6.4 Echoes and Reflection

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how echoes are formed due to the reflection of sound waves.

Key concepts:

  • Reflection of Sound: Sound waves can bounce off surfaces, similar to how light reflects off a mirror.
  • Echo: An echo is a reflected sound wave that is heard shortly after the original sound.
  • Conditions for hearing an echo:
    • There must be a large, hard, flat surface for the sound to reflect off.
    • The listener must be a sufficient distance from the reflecting surface for the original sound and the reflected sound to be distinguishable (usually at least 17 meters for a clear echo, as the human ear needs about 0.1 seconds to distinguish between two sounds).
  • Applications of echoes:
    • SONAR (Sound Navigation and Ranging) used by ships and submarines to detect objects underwater and measure depth.
    • Ultrasound imaging in medicine.
    • Echolocation used by animals like bats and dolphins.
  • Soft surfaces absorb sound and reduce echoes (e.g., curtains, carpets in a room).
