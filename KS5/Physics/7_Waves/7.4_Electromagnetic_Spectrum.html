7.4 The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Learning Objectives:

  1. State that all electromagnetic waves are transverse waves that travel with the same speed c in free space.
  2. Recall the approximate range of wavelengths in free space of the principal regions of the electromagnetic spectrum from radio waves to $\gamma$-rays.
  3. Recall that wavelengths in the range 400-700 nm in free space are visible to the human eye.

LO1: Properties of Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic (EM) waves are created by oscillating electric and magnetic fields. They are a family of waves that share several fundamental properties:

  • They are all transverse waves. The oscillations of the electric and magnetic fields are perpendicular to each other and also perpendicular to the direction of energy propagation.
  • They all travel at the same speed in a vacuum (free space). This speed is known as the speed of light, $c$, and has a value of approximately $3.00 \times 10^8$ m s⁻¹.
  • They can travel through a vacuum. Unlike mechanical waves (like sound), they do not require a medium to propagate.

LO2 & LO3: Regions of the Spectrum

The electromagnetic spectrum is the continuous range of all possible frequencies (and wavelengths) of electromagnetic radiation. We divide this spectrum into principal regions based on wavelength, although the boundaries are not sharply defined. The different regions have different sources and applications.

Since all EM waves travel at the same speed ($c$), the wave equation $c = f\lambda$ shows that wavelength and frequency are inversely proportional. Waves with long wavelengths have low frequencies, and waves with short wavelengths have high frequencies.

Region Approximate Wavelength Range (in a vacuum)
Radio waves> 10 cm
Microwaves1 mm – 10 cm
Infrared700 nm – 1 mm
Visible light400 nm – 700 nm
Ultraviolet10 nm – 400 nm
X-rays0.01 nm – 10 nm
Gamma ($\gamma$) rays< 0.01 nm

The only part of this vast spectrum that is visible to the human eye is a very narrow band of wavelengths from approximately **400 nm (violet)** to **700 nm (red)**.

Worked Example: Calculating Frequency

A green laser pointer emits light with a wavelength of 532 nm. Calculate its frequency.

1. Identify variables and convert to SI units:

$$ \lambda = 532 \text{ nm} = 532 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} $$

$$ c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1} $$

2. Rearrange the wave equation for frequency:

$$ c = f\lambda \implies f = \frac{c}{\lambda} $$

3. Substitute values and solve:

$$ f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8}{532 \times 10^{-9}} $$

$$ f = 5.64 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz} $$
