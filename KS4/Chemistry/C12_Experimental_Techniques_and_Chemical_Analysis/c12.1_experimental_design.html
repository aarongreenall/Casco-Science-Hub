C12.1 Experimental design

Effective **experimental design** is the cornerstone of reliable scientific investigation. It involves carefully planning experiments to ensure that results are accurate, precise, and valid. A key aspect of this is selecting and using appropriate apparatus for measurements and understanding fundamental chemical terminology.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Name appropriate apparatus for the measurement of time, temperature, mass and volume, including: stop-watches, thermometers, balances, burettes, volumetric pipettes, measuring cylinders, gas syringes.
  2. Describe a: solvent as a substance that dissolves a solute; solute as a substance that is dissolved in a solvent; solution as a mixture of one or more solutes dissolved in a solvent; saturated solution as a solution containing the maximum concentration of a solute dissolved in the solvent at a specific temperature; residue as a solid substance that remains after evaporation, distillation, filtration or any similar process; filtrate as a liquid or solution that has passed through a filter.

Measuring Apparatus:

Accurate measurements are vital in chemistry. Various pieces of apparatus are used depending on the quantity being measured and the required precision:

  • **Time:** Measured using **stop-watches** (analogue or digital).
  • **Temperature:** Measured using **thermometers** (liquid-in-glass or digital).
  • **Mass:** Measured using **balances** (top-pan or analytical).
  • **Volume:** Measured using a variety of apparatus, each with different levels of precision: * **Measuring cylinders:** For approximate volumes of liquids. * **Burettes:** For precise measurement of variable volumes of liquids (e.g., in titrations). * **Volumetric pipettes:** For very precise measurement of fixed volumes of liquids. * **Gas syringes:** For measuring volumes of gases.

Key Chemical Terminology: Solutions and Separations:

Understanding precise terminology is crucial in chemistry, especially when dealing with solutions and separation techniques:

  • **Solvent:** A substance that dissolves a solute to form a solution (e.g., water in salt water).
  • **Solute:** A substance that is dissolved in a solvent (e.g., salt in salt water).
  • **Solution:** A homogeneous mixture formed when one or more solutes are dissolved in a solvent (e.g., salt water).
  • **Saturated Solution:** A solution containing the maximum concentration of a solute that can be dissolved in a given solvent at a specific temperature. No more solute can dissolve at that temperature.
  • **Residue:** A solid substance that remains on the filter paper after filtration, or in the distillation flask after evaporation/distillation.
  • **Filtrate:** The liquid or solution that has passed through a filter during filtration.
