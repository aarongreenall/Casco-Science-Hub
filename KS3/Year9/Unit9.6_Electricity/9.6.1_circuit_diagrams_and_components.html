9.6.1 Circuit Diagrams and Components

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page reviews and expands on the use of standard symbols to represent electrical circuits.

Recap of Common Circuit Symbols:

Component Symbol Function
Wire────Allows current to flow.
Cell─|│─Provides voltage.
Battery─|│|│─A combination of cells.
Switch──/ ──Opens or closes the circuit.
Bulb / Lamp─(X)─Converts electrical energy to light.
Ammeter─(A)─Measures current.
Voltmeter─(V)─Measures voltage (potential difference).
Resistor─[ ]─Resists the flow of current.

New Components:

Component Symbol Function
Variable Resistor─[↗]─Allows resistance to be changed.
Diode─▷|─Allows current to flow in one direction only.
LED (Light Emitting Diode)─▷|↗─A diode that emits light when current flows through it.
Fuse─[––]─A safety device that melts and breaks the circuit if the current is too high.

Being able to draw and interpret circuit diagrams is a fundamental skill in physics.
