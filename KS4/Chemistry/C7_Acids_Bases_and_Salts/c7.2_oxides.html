C7.2 Oxides

Oxides are chemical compounds that contain at least one oxygen atom and one other element in their chemical formula. They are a very common and diverse class of compounds, and their chemical properties (whether they are acidic, basic, or amphoteric) are directly related to the metallic or non-metallic character of the element bonded to oxygen.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Classify oxides as acidic, including SO$_2$ and CO$_2$, or basic, including CuO and CaO, related to metallic and non-metallic character.
  2. Describe amphoteric oxides as oxides that react with acids and with bases to produce a salt and water.
  3. Classify Al$_2$O$_3$ and ZnO as amphoteric oxides.

Classifying Oxides:

Oxides can be classified into three main types based on their chemical behavior:

1. Acidic Oxides:

**Acidic oxides** are typically formed from **non-metallic elements**. They react with bases (or alkalis) to form a salt and water. When dissolved in water, they often form acidic solutions.

  • **Examples:** Sulfur dioxide (SO$_2$), Carbon dioxide (CO$_2$).
  • `SO2(g) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2SO3(aq) + H2O(l)`
  • `CO2(g) + 2NaOH(aq) -> Na2CO3(aq) + H2O(l)`

*Figure: Bubbling carbon dioxide through limewater (calcium hydroxide solution) turns it cloudy, indicating an acidic oxide reacting with a base.*

2. Basic Oxides:

**Basic oxides** are typically formed from **metallic elements**. They react with acids to form a salt and water. Soluble basic oxides are called alkalis.

  • **Examples:** Copper(II) oxide (CuO), Calcium oxide (CaO), Sodium oxide (Na$_2$O).
  • `CuO(s) + H2SO4(aq) -> CuSO4(aq) + H2O(l)`
  • `CaO(s) + 2HCl(aq) -> CaCl2(aq) + H2O(l)`

*Figure: Copper(II) oxide, a basic oxide, typically a black solid.*

3. Amphoteric Oxides:

**Amphoteric oxides** are a special class of oxides that can react with **both acids and bases** to produce a salt and water. They exhibit both acidic and basic properties.

  • **Examples:** Aluminum oxide (Al$_2$O$_3$), Zinc oxide (ZnO).
  • **Reaction with Acid (acting as a base):** * `Al2O3(s) + 6HCl(aq) -> 2AlCl3(aq) + 3H2O(l)`
  • **Reaction with Base (acting as an acid):** * `Al2O3(s) + 2NaOH(aq) -> 2NaAlO2(aq) + H2O(l)` (Sodium aluminate is a salt)

*Figure: Aluminum oxide, a compound that exhibits amphoteric properties.*
