C10.2 Air quality and climate

The quality of the air we breathe and the stability of our climate are critical environmental concerns. Human activities, particularly the combustion of fossil fuels and industrial processes, release various pollutants into the atmosphere, impacting both local air quality and global climate patterns. Understanding the composition of clean air, the sources and effects of pollutants, and strategies to mitigate their impact is essential for environmental stewardship.

Learning Objectives:

  1. State the composition of clean, dry air as approximately 78% nitrogen, N$_2$, 21% oxygen, O$_2$ and the remainder as a mixture of noble gases and carbon dioxide, CO$_2$.
  2. State the source of each of these air pollutants, limited to: carbon dioxide from the complete combustion of carbon-containing fuels; carbon monoxide and particulates from the incomplete combustion of carbon-containing fuels; methane from the decomposition of vegetation and waste gases from digestion in animals; oxides of nitrogen from car engines; sulfur dioxide from the combustion of fossil fuels which contain sulfur compounds.
  3. State the adverse effect of these air pollutants, limited to: carbon dioxide: higher levels of carbon dioxide leading to increased global warming, which leads to climate change; carbon monoxide: toxic gas; particulates: increased risk of respiratory problems and cancer; methane: higher levels of methane leading to increased global warming, which leads to climate change; oxides of nitrogen: acid rain and respiratory problems; sulfur dioxide: acid rain.
  4. Describe how the greenhouse gases carbon dioxide and methane cause global warming, limited to: the absorption, reflection and emission of thermal energy; reducing thermal energy loss to space.
  5. State and explain strategies to reduce the effects of climate change: planting trees; reduction in livestock farming; decreasing use of fossil fuels; increasing use of hydrogen and renewable energy, e.g. wind, solar.
  6. State and explain strategies to reduce the effects of acid rain: reducing emissions of sulfur dioxide by using low-sulfur fuels and flue gas desulfurisation with calcium oxide.
  7. Explain how oxides of nitrogen form in car engines and describe their removal by catalytic converters, limited to: $2CO + 2NO \rightarrow 2CO_2 + N_2$.

Composition of Clean, Dry Air:

**Clean, dry air** is a mixture of several gases. Its approximate composition is:

  • Approximately **78% Nitrogen (N$_2$)**
  • Approximately **21% Oxygen (O$_2$)**
  • The remaining 1% is a mixture of **noble gases** (primarily Argon) and **carbon dioxide (CO$_2$)**.

Air Pollutants: Sources and Adverse Effects:

Human activities introduce various pollutants into the atmosphere, each with specific sources and harmful effects:

  • **Carbon Dioxide (CO$_2$):** * **Source:** Complete combustion of carbon-containing fuels (e.g., fossil fuels like coal, oil, natural gas). * **Adverse Effect:** Higher levels lead to increased **global warming**, which causes **climate change**.
  • **Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Particulates:** * **Source:** Incomplete combustion of carbon-containing fuels (occurs when there is insufficient oxygen for complete burning). * **Adverse Effect (CO):** Highly **toxic gas**; binds to hemoglobin in red blood cells, reducing oxygen transport. * **Adverse Effect (Particulates):** Tiny solid particles (like soot) that increase the risk of **respiratory problems and cancer** when inhaled.
  • **Methane (CH$_4$):** * **Source:** Decomposition of vegetation (e.g., in landfills, wetlands) and waste gases from digestion in animals (e.g., livestock farming). * **Adverse Effect:** Higher levels lead to increased **global warming**, contributing to **climate change**.
  • **Oxides of Nitrogen (NOx, e.g., NO, NO$_2$):** * **Source:** Formed in **car engines** (and power stations) at high temperatures, where nitrogen and oxygen from the air react. * **Adverse Effect:** Contribute to **acid rain** and cause **respiratory problems**.
  • **Sulfur Dioxide (SO$_2$):** * **Source:** Combustion of fossil fuels (especially coal and some crude oil) which contain sulfur compounds. * **Adverse Effect:** Major cause of **acid rain**.

*Figure: Common sources of air pollution from human activities.*

Global Warming and Climate Change (Greenhouse Effect):

**Global warming** is the observed increase in the Earth's average surface temperature, primarily due to the increased concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. This warming leads to **climate change**, which encompasses broader shifts in weather patterns and related impacts.

**Greenhouse gases** like carbon dioxide (CO$_2$) and methane (CH$_4$) cause global warming through the **greenhouse effect**:

  • The Earth absorbs short-wavelength radiation (visible light) from the Sun, which warms its surface.
  • The Earth then emits longer-wavelength thermal energy (infrared radiation) back into space.
  • Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere **absorb** some of this outgoing infrared radiation, **reflecting** some of it back towards Earth and **emitting** some in all directions.
  • This process traps thermal energy, **reducing thermal energy loss to space** and leading to an increase in global temperatures.

*Figure: Diagram illustrating the greenhouse effect, showing how greenhouse gases trap thermal energy.*

Strategies to Reduce Environmental Impacts:

Strategies to Reduce Climate Change:

  • **Planting Trees:** Trees absorb carbon dioxide during photosynthesis, helping to remove it from the atmosphere.
  • **Reduction in Livestock Farming:** Livestock (especially cattle) produce significant amounts of methane through digestion. Reducing consumption of meat and dairy can lower methane emissions.
  • **Decreasing Use of Fossil Fuels:** Shifting away from burning coal, oil, and natural gas for energy production reduces carbon dioxide emissions.
  • **Increasing Use of Hydrogen and Renewable Energy:** Promoting energy sources like wind, solar, hydroelectric, and hydrogen fuel cells (which produce only water) reduces reliance on fossil fuels.

Strategies to Reduce Acid Rain:

Acid rain is primarily caused by sulfur dioxide and oxides of nitrogen. Strategies to reduce it include:

  • **Reducing Emissions of Sulfur Dioxide:** * **Using low-sulfur fuels:** Burning fuels with lower sulfur content. * **Flue gas desulfurisation:** Removing sulfur dioxide from power station exhaust gases using calcium oxide (limestone) or calcium carbonate. * `CaO(s) + SO2(g) -> CaSO3(s)` (Calcium sulfite)
  • **Reducing Emissions of Oxides of Nitrogen:** * **Catalytic converters** in car engines convert harmful nitrogen oxides and carbon monoxide into less harmful nitrogen and carbon dioxide. * `2CO(g) + 2NO(g) -> 2CO2(g) + N2(g)`
