7.2.1 Atoms and elements

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define an atom as the smallest particle of an element.
  2. Define an element as a pure substance made up of only one type of atom.
  3. Describe the structure of an atom in terms of protons, neutrons, and electrons.

Atoms and Elements

Everything in the universe, from the air you breathe to the stars in the sky, is made of matter. The fundamental building blocks of this matter are called **atoms**. An **element** is a pure substance that is made up of only one type of atom. For example, a pure gold bar contains only gold atoms, and a balloon filled with helium contains only helium atoms.

Analogy: LEGO Bricks

Think about a giant box of LEGO bricks. You might have red bricks, blue bricks, and yellow bricks. Each colour represents a different **element**. All the red 2x4 bricks are the element "Red Brickium," and all the blue 2x4 bricks are the element "Blue Brickium." A single, individual red brick is therefore an **atom** of Red Brickium.

Inside an Atom

Atoms themselves are made of even smaller particles called **subatomic particles**. There are three types you need to know:

Protons: Found in the centre of the atom, in a region called the **nucleus**. They have a positive (+) electric charge.

Neutrons: Also found in the nucleus. They have no charge (they are neutral).

Electrons: They move around the nucleus in specific energy levels called shells. They have a negative (-) electric charge.

In-School Activity: Exploring the Periodic Table

The number of protons in an atom defines what element it is. This is called the **atomic number**. In a neutral atom, the number of electrons is equal to the number of protons. Use a periodic table to find the atomic number for the first 20 elements and complete a table showing the number of protons and electrons in a neutral atom of each.
