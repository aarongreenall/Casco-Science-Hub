C4.2 Hydrogen fuel cells

Hydrogen fuel cells are electrochemical devices that convert the chemical energy of hydrogen and oxygen directly into electrical energy, producing water as the only chemical product. They offer a promising alternative to traditional combustion engines and batteries, especially in the context of clean energy and sustainable transportation.

Learning Objectives:

  1. State that a hydrogen–oxygen fuel cell uses hydrogen and oxygen to produce electricity with water as the only chemical product.
  2. Describe the advantages and disadvantages of using hydrogen–oxygen fuel cells in comparison with gasoline/petrol engines in vehicles.

How Hydrogen-Oxygen Fuel Cells Work:

A **hydrogen-oxygen fuel cell** operates by combining hydrogen gas (H$_2$) and oxygen gas (O$_2$) to produce electricity, with water (H$_2$O) as the sole chemical byproduct. This process is essentially the reverse of the electrolysis of water.

The fuel cell has two electrodes (anode and cathode) separated by an electrolyte.

  • **At the anode (negative electrode):** Hydrogen gas is supplied and loses electrons (oxidized) to form hydrogen ions. * $2H_2(g) \rightarrow 4H^+(aq) + 4e^-$
  • **At the cathode (positive electrode):** Oxygen gas is supplied and gains electrons (reduced) from the external circuit, reacting with hydrogen ions to form water. * $O_2(g) + 4H^+(aq) + 4e^- \rightarrow 2H_2O(l)$
  • **Overall reaction:** The electrons flow from the anode to the cathode through an external circuit, generating an electric current. * $2H_2(g) + O_2(g) \rightarrow 2H_2O(l)$

Advantages and Disadvantages in Vehicles:

Hydrogen-oxygen fuel cells are being developed as a clean energy source for vehicles, offering several advantages and disadvantages compared to traditional gasoline/petrol engines:

Advantages:

  • **Clean Emissions:** The only chemical product is water, meaning no greenhouse gases (like carbon dioxide) or harmful pollutants (like nitrogen oxides or sulfur dioxide) are produced during operation.
  • **High Efficiency:** Fuel cells convert chemical energy directly into electrical energy, often with higher efficiency than combustion engines.
  • **Quiet Operation:** They operate very quietly compared to internal combustion engines.
  • **Renewable Hydrogen Source:** If hydrogen is produced from renewable energy sources (e.g., electrolysis of water using solar power), the entire cycle can be carbon-neutral.

Disadvantages:

  • **Hydrogen Production:** Producing hydrogen often requires energy, and if this energy comes from fossil fuels, the overall process is not entirely clean.
  • **Storage and Distribution:** Hydrogen is a gas at room temperature and requires high pressure or very low temperatures to store, making storage and distribution challenging and expensive.
  • **Cost:** Fuel cell technology and hydrogen infrastructure are currently more expensive than traditional gasoline/petrol systems.
  • **Limited Infrastructure:** The network of hydrogen refueling stations is currently very limited compared to petrol stations.
