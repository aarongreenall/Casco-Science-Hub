7.7.7 Food chains and webs with decomposers

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define a food chain and a food web.
  2. Identify producers, consumers, and decomposers in a food web.
  3. Explain the essential role of decomposers in recycling nutrients.

Who Eats Whom?

In any ecosystem, energy flows from one organism to another. We can show this flow of energy using food chains and food webs.

  • A **food chain** is a simple diagram that shows a single path of energy flow. The arrow always points from the organism being eaten to the organism that eats it.
  • A **food web** is a more realistic diagram that shows how multiple food chains are interconnected in an ecosystem.

In any food web, organisms have specific roles:

  • Producers: Green plants that produce their own food using energy from the sun (photosynthesis). They are the start of every food chain.
  • Consumers: Animals that get their energy by eating other organisms. Primary consumers eat producers, secondary consumers eat primary consumers, and so on.

The Final, Essential Link

What happens when the producers and consumers die? Their bodies contain valuable nutrients. This is where the **decomposers** come in. Decomposers, mainly bacteria and fungi, are the final link in the food web. They break down all dead organic materialâ€”dead plants, dead animals, and waste products like droppings.

Analogy: The Ultimate Recycling Crew

Decomposers are the **"Ultimate Recycling Crew"** of the ecosystem. They take all the "waste" (dead organisms) and break it down into simple, useful raw materials (nutrients). These nutrients are returned to the soil, where they can be used by the producers to grow. Without decomposers, the flow of nutrients would stop, and the whole ecosystem would collapse.

In-School Activity: Build a Food Web

Your teacher will give you a set of cards, each with a different organism from a woodland ecosystem (e.g., oak tree, grass, caterpillar, rabbit, fox, owl, bacteria, fungi).

Your Task:
  1. First, arrange some of the cards to make a simple food chain. Remember to draw arrows to show the direction of energy flow.
  2. Next, use all the cards to create a more complex food web, showing how the different food chains are interconnected.
  3. Finally, add the decomposer cards (bacteria and fungi). Draw arrows from all the other organisms to the decomposers to show that they break down everything when it dies.
