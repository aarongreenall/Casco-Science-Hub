B8.1 Xylem and phloem

Plants have specialized transport tissues, collectively known as **vascular bundles**, that are essential for their survival. These tissues, primarily **xylem** and **phloem**, form a sophisticated network throughout the plant, enabling the efficient movement of water, mineral ions, and manufactured food substances.

Learning Objectives:

  1. State the functions of xylem and phloem: xylem – transport of water and mineral ions, and support; phloem – transport of sucrose and amino acids.
  2. Identify in diagrams and images the position of xylem and phloem as seen in sections of roots, stems and leaves of non-woody dicotyledonous plants.

Functions of Xylem and Phloem:

Xylem and phloem are the two main types of transport tissues in plants, each with distinct roles:

  • **Xylem:** * **Function 1: Transport of Water and Mineral Ions:** Xylem vessels form a continuous tube from the roots, through the stem, to the leaves. They transport water (absorbed by root hairs) and dissolved mineral ions upwards to all parts of the plant. * **Function 2: Support:** The walls of xylem vessels are strengthened with lignin, providing structural support to the plant, helping it stand upright.
  • **Phloem:** * **Function: Transport of Sucrose and Amino Acids:** Phloem vessels transport manufactured food substances, primarily **sucrose** (a sugar produced during photosynthesis) and **amino acids**, from the sites of photosynthesis (sources, mainly leaves) to other parts of the plant where they are needed for growth or storage (sinks, e.g., roots, fruits, growing tips). This process is called translocation.

*Figure: Diagram illustrating the functions of xylem (water and mineral transport) and phloem (sucrose and amino acid transport).*

Position in Roots, Stems, and Leaves:

The arrangement of xylem and phloem within vascular bundles varies in different plant organs:

  • **Roots:** Xylem is typically in the center, forming a star shape, with phloem bundles located between the arms of the xylem. This central arrangement provides strength against pulling forces.
  • **Stems (non-woody dicotyledonous):** Xylem and phloem are arranged in vascular bundles around the periphery of the stem, with xylem usually towards the inside and phloem towards the outside. This arrangement provides support and efficient transport.
  • **Leaves:** Xylem and phloem are found together in the veins of the leaf, forming a network that distributes water and collects sugars. Xylem is typically on the upper side of the vein, and phloem on the lower side.
