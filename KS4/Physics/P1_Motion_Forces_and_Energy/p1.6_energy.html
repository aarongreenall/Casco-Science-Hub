P1.6 Energy

Energy is a fundamental concept in physics, representing the capacity to do work or cause change. It exists in various forms and can be converted from one form to another, but it is always conserved. Understanding energy is crucial for explaining everything from the movement of planets to the operation of everyday devices.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Recall and use the principle of the conservation of energy.
  2. State that energy is transferred from one form to another, and that no energy is lost.
  3. Recall and use the equations for kinetic energy ($E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and gravitational potential energy ($E_p = mgh$).
  4. Describe how energy is converted from one form to another, including examples of common energy conversions.
  5. Recall and use the equation for work done ($W = Fd$).
  6. Define power as work done per unit time or energy transferred per unit time; recall and use the equation $P = W/t$ and $P = E/t$.
  7. Recall and use the equation for efficiency ($Efficiency = \frac{Useful \ energy \ output}{Total \ energy \ input} \times 100\%$).

Forms of Energy and Conservation:

Energy exists in many different forms, including:

  • **Kinetic energy ($E_k$):** Energy due to motion.
  • **Gravitational potential energy ($E_p$):** Energy due to an object's position in a gravitational field.
  • **Elastic potential energy:** Energy stored in stretched or compressed elastic materials.
  • **Chemical energy:** Energy stored in the bonds of chemical compounds.
  • **Thermal (heat) energy:** Energy associated with the random motion of atoms and molecules.
  • **Electrical energy:** Energy associated with the flow of electric charge.
  • **Nuclear energy:** Energy stored in the nucleus of atoms.
  • **Light energy:** Energy carried by electromagnetic waves.
  • **Sound energy:** Energy carried by vibrations.

The **Principle of Conservation of Energy** is one of the most fundamental laws in physics. It states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred from one form to another. In any closed system, the total amount of energy remains constant. For example, when a ball is dropped, its gravitational potential energy is converted into kinetic energy as it falls. When it hits the ground, this kinetic energy is converted into sound and thermal energy.

Work Done, Power, and Efficiency:

**Work done ($W$)** is a measure of the energy transferred when a force causes an object to move a certain distance. It is calculated by the equation:

$W = F \times d$

Where $F$ is the force applied and $d$ is the distance moved in the direction of the force. Work done is measured in Joules (J).

This video from **Cognitoedu** (3:54 min) explains the concepts of work done.

**Power ($P$)** is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. It tells us how quickly energy is being used or converted. The equations for power are:

$P = \frac{W}{t}$ or $P = \frac{E}{t}$

This video from **Cognitoedu** (3:54 min) explains the concept of power in physics.

**Efficiency** is a measure of how effectively energy is converted from one form to another in a system or device. No energy conversion is 100% efficient; some energy is always "wasted," usually as thermal energy (heat). Efficiency is calculated as:

$Efficiency = \frac{Useful \ energy \ output}{Total \ energy \ input} \times 100\%$
