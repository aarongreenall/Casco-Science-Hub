8.5.3 Chemical Equations and Products

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how to predict the products of reactions and represent them using word equations.

General Reaction Types:

  • Metal + Acid → Salt + Hydrogen
    • Example: Zinc + Hydrochloric Acid → Zinc Chloride + Hydrogen
  • Metal Carbonate + Acid → Salt + Water + Carbon Dioxide
    • Example: Calcium Carbonate + Sulfuric Acid → Calcium Sulfate + Water + Carbon Dioxide
  • Metal Oxide (Base) + Acid → Salt + Water (Neutralisation)
    • Example: Copper Oxide + Nitric Acid → Copper Nitrate + Water
  • Alkali (Soluble Base) + Acid → Salt + Water (Neutralisation)
    • Example: Sodium Hydroxide + Hydrochloric Acid → Sodium Chloride + Water

Naming Salts:

  • The first part of the salt's name comes from the metal in the base, alkali, or carbonate.
  • The second part of the name comes from the acid used:
    • Hydrochloric Acid → ... chloride
    • Sulfuric Acid → ... sulfate
    • Nitric Acid → ... nitrate

Word Equations:

  • A way to represent a chemical reaction using the names of the substances.
  • Reactants are written on the left, products on the right, separated by an arrow (→) which means "react to form".
  • Example: Reactant 1 + Reactant 2 → Product 1 + Product 2
