8.5.1 Atomic Structure (Rutherford Model)

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will describe the Rutherford-Bohr model of the atom, including its main components.

The Atomic Model:

  • An atom consists of a central nucleus and electrons that orbit the nucleus.
  • The atom is mostly empty space.

Subatomic Particles:

Particle Location Relative Mass Relative Charge
Proton Nucleus 1 +1
Neutron Nucleus 1 0
Electron Shells/Orbits ~1/1840 (very small) -1

Key Atomic Numbers:

  • Atomic Number (Z): The number of protons in the nucleus. This defines the element (e.g., any atom with 6 protons is a carbon atom). In a neutral atom, the atomic number also equals the number of electrons.
  • Mass Number (A): The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.
  • Number of Neutrons = Mass Number - Atomic Number

Electron Arrangement:

  • Electrons are arranged in energy levels or shells around the nucleus.
  • The first shell can hold up to 2 electrons.
  • The second and third shells can hold up to 8 electrons each (for the first 20 elements).
  • The arrangement of electrons, particularly in the outermost shell, determines the chemical properties of an element.
