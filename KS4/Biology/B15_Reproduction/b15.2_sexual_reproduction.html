B15.2 Sexual reproduction

**Sexual reproduction** is a type of reproduction that involves the fusion of male and female gametes (sex cells) to form a zygote. This process leads to offspring that are genetically different from both parents, introducing genetic variation within a population. Understanding sexual reproduction is crucial for comprehending evolution, inheritance, and the diversity of life.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe sexual reproduction as reproduction involving the fusion of gametes, producing genetically dissimilar offspring.
  2. State that sexual reproduction involves meiosis.
  3. Describe the advantages and disadvantages of sexual reproduction.

Understanding Sexual Reproduction:

**Sexual reproduction** is a type of reproduction that involves:

  • **Two parents** (typically, though some organisms are hermaphroditic).
  • The production and fusion of specialized sex cells called **gametes** (sperm and egg in animals, pollen and ovule in plants).
  • The fusion of gametes (fertilization) forms a **zygote**, which develops into a new individual.
  • Offspring are **genetically dissimilar** to both parents, as they inherit a mix of genetic information from each.

Sexual reproduction involves a type of cell division called **meiosis**. Meiosis produces gametes with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell (haploid), ensuring that when two gametes fuse, the resulting zygote has the correct diploid number of chromosomes.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Sexual Reproduction:

Sexual reproduction offers distinct advantages and disadvantages compared to asexual reproduction:

Advantages:

  • **Genetic variation:** Produces offspring that are genetically different from parents. This **variation** increases the chances that some individuals in a population will survive if environmental conditions change (e.g., new disease, climate change). This is crucial for evolution and adaptation.
  • **Adaptation:** Allows populations to adapt to changing environments over time.

Disadvantages:

  • **Requires two parents:** Organisms need to find a mate, which can be challenging for isolated species.
  • **Slower process:** Typically takes more time and energy compared to asexual reproduction.
  • **Favorable traits may be lost:** Desirable traits from parents might not be passed on to all offspring due to genetic recombination.
