18.4 Electric field of a point charge

Learning Objectives:

  1. Recall and use $E = \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2}$ for the electric field strength due to a point charge in free space.

LO1: Electric Field Strength of a Point Charge

We can determine the strength of the electric field created by a single point charge, $Q$, by combining two fundamental equations: the definition of electric field strength and Coulomb's Law.

Detailed Derivation of $E = \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2}$

1. Start with the definition of electric field strength, $E$. It is the force $F$ experienced by a small, positive test charge $q$, divided by the magnitude of that test charge.

$$ E = \frac{F}{q} $$

2. The force $F$ between the source charge $Q$ and the test charge $q$ is given by Coulomb's Law:

$$ F = \frac{Qq}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2} $$

3. Substitute the expression for the force $F$ from Coulomb's Law into the definition of electric field strength:

$$ E = \frac{\left(\frac{Qq}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2}\right)}{q} $$

4. The test charge $q$ in the numerator and the denominator cancels out:

$$ E = \frac{Q\cancel{q}}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2 \cancel{q}} $$

5. This leaves the final formula for the electric field strength due to a point charge $Q$:

$$ E = \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2} $$

This is a powerful result because it shows that the field strength at a point depends only on the source charge ($Q$) that creates the field and the distance from it ($r$), not on the test charge placed in the field.

Worked Example: Field Strength of a Proton

Calculate the magnitude of the electric field strength at a distance of 53 pm (the Bohr radius) from a single proton.

1. Identify variables and convert to SI units:

The source charge is the proton, so $Q = +e = +1.60 \times 10^{-19}$ C.

$$ r = 53 \text{ pm} = 53 \times 10^{-12} \text{ m} $$

2. State the formula and substitute values:

$$ E = \frac{Q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r^2} $$

$$ E = \frac{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}{4\pi(8.85 \times 10^{-12})(53 \times 10^{-12})^2} $$

3. Calculate the result:

$$ E = \frac{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}{3.11 \times 10^{-31}} $$

$$ E = 5.14 \times 10^{11} \text{ N C}^{-1} $$
