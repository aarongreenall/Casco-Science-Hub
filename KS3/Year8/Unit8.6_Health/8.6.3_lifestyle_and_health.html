8.6.3 Lifestyle and health

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define lifestyle as the choices we make about how we live.
  2. Explain how diet can affect health, leading to conditions like obesity and type 2 diabetes.
  3. Describe the harmful effects of smoking on the lungs and circulatory system.

Your Choices Matter

Our health is not just about avoiding germs. It is also hugely affected by our **lifestyle**â€”the choices we make every day about what we eat, how much we exercise, and what we put into our bodies. Making healthy choices can help us live longer, healthier lives, while making unhealthy choices can lead to serious diseases.

Analogy: Car Maintenance

Think of your body like a high-performance car. To keep it working well, you need to look after it. **Diet** is like the fuel you put in. If you use the correct, high-quality fuel (a balanced diet), the car will run smoothly for a long time. If you fill it with the wrong fuel (junk food), the engine will get clogged up and break down. **Smoking** is like deliberately pouring sand and tar into the engine and exhaust pipe. It will cause serious, long-term damage and eventually lead to a total breakdown.

Diet and Smoking

The Impact of Diet

Eating too much food, especially food high in fat and sugar, without doing enough exercise, leads to the excess energy being stored as fat. This can lead to **obesity**, which puts a strain on the heart and can cause serious health problems like **type 2 diabetes** and heart disease.

The Dangers of Smoking

Tobacco smoke contains thousands of harmful chemicals. **Tar** is a sticky, black substance that coats the lungs, damages the alveoli, and can cause lung cancer. **Nicotine** is an addictive drug that increases heart rate and blood pressure. **Carbon monoxide** is a poisonous gas that reduces the amount of oxygen your red blood cells can carry.

Explanation: Health Risks of Smoking

In-School Activity: Health Campaign Poster

In small groups, choose one lifestyle-related health issue (e.g., "The effects of a high-sugar diet," "Smoking and lung cancer," or "The benefits of regular exercise").

Your Task:

Use textbooks and reliable internet sources to research your chosen topic. Create an informative poster for a health campaign aimed at other students. Your poster should include:

  • A clear, catchy title.
  • A simple explanation of the science behind the issue.
  • At least three key facts or statistics.
  • Clear diagrams or images.
  • Advice on how to make healthier choices.
