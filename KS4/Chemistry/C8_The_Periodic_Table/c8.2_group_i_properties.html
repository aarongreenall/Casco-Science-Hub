C8.2 Group I properties

Group I elements, also known as the **alkali metals**, are located on the far left of the Periodic Table. They include lithium (Li), sodium (Na), and potassium (K). These elements share characteristic physical and chemical properties due to having only one electron in their outermost shell, which they readily lose to form positive ions.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the Group I alkali metals, lithium, sodium and potassium, as relatively soft metals with general trends down the group, limited to: decreasing melting point; increasing density; increasing reactivity with water.
  2. Predict the properties of other elements in Group I, given information about the elements.

Physical Properties of Alkali Metals:

The alkali metals are relatively soft metals that can be cut with a knife. They are shiny when freshly cut but quickly tarnish upon exposure to air due to reaction with oxygen.

As you move **down Group I** (from lithium to potassium):

  • **Melting Point:** Decreases. The metallic bonding forces become weaker as the atomic radius increases.
  • **Density:** Increases. Atoms become heavier and larger, but the increase in mass outweighs the increase in volume.

*Figure: Trends in melting point and density down Group I alkali metals.*

Chemical Properties and Reactivity with Water:

Alkali metals are highly reactive because they readily lose their single outermost electron to form a +1 ion. Their chemical properties are largely determined by this tendency.

As you move **down Group I**, the **reactivity with water increases**. This is because:

  • The atomic radius increases, meaning the outermost electron is further from the positively charged nucleus.
  • The shielding effect from inner electron shells increases.
  • Less energy is required to remove the outermost electron, making the atom more reactive.

Alkali metals react vigorously with cold water to produce a metal hydroxide (an alkali) and hydrogen gas. The reaction becomes more vigorous down the group:

  • **Lithium:** Fizzes steadily, moves on water surface.
  • **Sodium:** Melts into a sphere, fizzes rapidly, moves quickly on water surface.
  • **Potassium:** Melts, fizzes very vigorously, moves very rapidly, often ignites with a lilac flame.

`2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)`
