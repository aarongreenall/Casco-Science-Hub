7.9.4 Components Affecting Current

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how different components in a circuit can affect the size of the electric current.

Key concepts:

  • Voltage: The "push" or "pressure" from the cell or battery that makes the current flow. Measured in Volts (V).
    • Increasing the voltage (e.g., adding more cells in series) will increase the current.
  • Resistance: A measure of how much a component opposes the flow of electric current. Measured in Ohms (Î©).
    • Components with high resistance reduce the current.
    • Components with low resistance allow a larger current to flow.
  • Effect of components on current in a series circuit:
    • Adding more bulbs in series increases the total resistance of the circuit, which decreases the current. This is why the bulbs become dimmer.
    • Using longer wires or thinner wires can also increase resistance and decrease the current.
  • A simple relationship: For a given voltage, a higher resistance leads to a lower current.
