8.6.1 Balanced Diet and Nutrients

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain the importance of a balanced diet and the roles of the main nutrient groups.

A Balanced Diet:

A balanced diet provides all the essential nutrients in the correct proportions to keep the body healthy and functioning correctly. The energy you get from food should be balanced with the energy you use.

Seven Main Nutrient Groups:

Nutrient Group Main Function(s) Food Sources
Carbohydrates Provide energy. (Starch for slow release, sugars for quick release). Bread, pasta, rice, potatoes, sugary foods.
Proteins Growth and repair of tissues. Meat, fish, eggs, beans, lentils, nuts.
Fats (Lipids) Provide a store of energy, insulation, and protection for organs. Butter, oil, nuts, cheese, fatty meat.
Vitamins Needed in small amounts for various metabolic processes (e.g., Vitamin C for healthy skin, Vitamin D for bones). Fruits and vegetables.
Minerals Needed in small amounts for healthy functioning (e.g., Calcium for bones, Iron for red blood cells). Fruits, vegetables, dairy, meat.
Fibre Aids digestion by helping food move through the gut. Wholegrain cereals, fruits, vegetables.
Water Essential for most bodily functions, including chemical reactions and transport. Drinks, fruits, vegetables.
