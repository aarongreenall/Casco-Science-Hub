C9.2 Uses of metals

The unique physical properties of metals make them indispensable in a vast array of applications, from everyday items to advanced technologies. Their strength, conductivity, and durability allow them to be used in ways that non-metals cannot. This section explores specific uses of common metals based on their characteristic properties.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the uses of metals in terms of their physical properties, including: aluminium in the manufacture of aircraft because of its low density; aluminium in the manufacture of overhead electrical cables because of its low density and good electrical conductivity; aluminium in food containers because of its resistance to corrosion; copper in electrical wiring because of its good electrical conductivity.

Applications of Metals Based on Properties:

Metals are selected for specific applications due to their advantageous physical properties:

Aluminium: Versatile and Lightweight:

**Aluminium (Al)** is a lightweight metal with excellent properties that make it highly versatile:

  • **Manufacture of Aircraft:** Aluminium's **low density** (lightweight) combined with its strength (especially in alloys) makes it ideal for aircraft construction, reducing fuel consumption and increasing payload capacity.
  • **Overhead Electrical Cables:** Aluminium is used for overhead electrical cables due to its **low density** (making long spans feasible) and **good electrical conductivity**. While copper is a better conductor, aluminium's lighter weight makes it more practical for long-distance power transmission.
  • **Food Containers:** Aluminium is used for food containers (e.g., cans, foil) primarily because of its excellent **resistance to corrosion**. It forms a thin, tough, self-protecting layer of aluminium oxide on its surface when exposed to air, preventing further reaction with food or liquids.

*Figure: Modern aircraft extensively use aluminum alloys for their lightweight and strong properties.*

Copper: The Electrical Workhorse:

**Copper (Cu)** is a highly valued metal, especially in electrical applications:

  • **Electrical Wiring:** Copper is extensively used in electrical wiring (e.g., in homes, appliances, electronic devices) because of its **excellent electrical conductivity**. It is one of the best conductors among common metals, allowing current to flow with minimal energy loss. It is also ductile, allowing it to be drawn into thin wires.

*Figure: Copper wires, commonly used in electrical installations due to their high conductivity.*
