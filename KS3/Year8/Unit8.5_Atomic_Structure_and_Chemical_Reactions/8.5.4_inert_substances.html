8.5.4 Inert substances

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define an inert substance as one that is chemically unreactive.
  2. Identify the noble gases as a group of inert elements.
  3. Explain how the unreactive nature of a substance can be a useful property.
  4. Describe the environmental problems caused by inert materials like plastics.

What Does "Inert" Mean?

While some elements like the alkali metals are extremely reactive, others are the complete opposite. A substance that does not easily take part in chemical reactions is described as **inert** or **unreactive**.

Analogy: The Finished LEGO Model

A reactive element, like sodium, is like a pile of loose LEGO bricks, ready and waiting to connect with other bricks. An inert substance, like a Noble Gas, is like a completed, perfect LEGO model. It's stable, complete, and has no need to connect with any other bricks. This is because its outer electron shell is already full.

Examples of Inert Substances

The most famous inert elements are the **Noble Gases** (Group 0 or 18) in the periodic table. Because they have a full outer shell of electrons, they are very stable and do not need to react to become stable. This is why helium is used in balloons instead of the highly reactive hydrogen.

Some metals, like **gold** and **platinum**, are also very unreactive. This is why they are used for jewelleryâ€”they don't rust or tarnish. Many modern materials, like **plastics**, are also designed to be inert. This is useful for food packaging because it means the plastic won't react with the food.

The Problem with Being Unreactive

The very property that makes plastics so usefulâ€”their inertnessâ€”also makes them a major environmental problem. Because they are so unreactive, they do not break down or decay naturally. They are not **biodegradable**. This means that almost every piece of plastic ever made still exists somewhere on Earth, often ending up in oceans and landfill sites where it can harm wildlife and ecosystems for hundreds of years.

In-School Activity: Research an Inert Substance

In small groups, choose one inert substance (e.g., Helium, Gold, or a type of plastic like PET). Research the substance and prepare a short presentation or poster for the class that answers the following questions:

  • What is it? (Element, compound, etc.)
  • What are its main properties?
  • What is it used for?
  • How does its unreactive (inert) nature make it suitable for these uses?
  • Does its inertness cause any problems (e.g., for the environment)?
