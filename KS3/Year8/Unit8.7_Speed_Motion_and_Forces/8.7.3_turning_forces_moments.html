8.7.3 Turning Forces (Moments)

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will introduce the concept of moments as turning forces and the principle of moments.

Moment:

  • A moment is the turning effect of a force.
  • It depends on two things: the size of the force and the distance from the pivot.
  • Formula: Moment = Force Ã— Perpendicular distance from the pivot
  • Units: Newton-meters (Nm).

Pivot (or Fulcrum):

  • The point around which an object turns.

Principle of Moments:

  • For an object to be balanced (in equilibrium), the total clockwise moment about a pivot must be equal to the total anticlockwise moment about the same pivot.
  • Formula: Total Clockwise Moments = Total Anticlockwise Moments

Levers:

  • Levers are simple machines that use moments to make work easier. They multiply the effect of a force.
  • Examples:
    • Seesaw: A classic example of the principle of moments.
    • Spanner: A long handle increases the distance from the pivot (the nut), so a smaller force is needed to create a large turning moment.
    • Crowbar: Used to lift heavy objects by applying a small force over a large distance.
    • Bottle opener, wheelbarrow, scissors.
