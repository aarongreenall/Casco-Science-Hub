8.8.1 Earth's Magnetic Field

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain the origin and importance of Earth's magnetic field.

What is the Magnetic Field?

  • Earth behaves like a giant bar magnet, with a north and a south magnetic pole.
  • It generates a magnetic field that extends from the Earth's interior out into space.
  • The magnetic field is thought to be generated by the movement of molten iron in the Earth's outer core (a process called the dynamo effect).

Importance of the Magnetic Field:

  • Navigation: A compass needle aligns itself with the Earth's magnetic field lines, pointing to the magnetic north pole. This has been crucial for navigation for centuries.
  • Protection from Solar Wind: The magnetic field (magnetosphere) deflects most of the charged particles from the solar wind, which would otherwise strip away our atmosphere.
  • Aurora Borealis and Australis: Some charged particles from the solar wind are trapped by the magnetic field and channeled towards the poles, where they collide with gas atoms in the atmosphere, creating the beautiful light displays known as the auroras.

Note: The geographic North Pole and the magnetic north pole are not in the exact same location.
