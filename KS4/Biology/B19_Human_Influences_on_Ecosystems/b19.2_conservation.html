B19.2 Conservation

**Conservation** is the careful management and protection of natural resources and ecosystems to prevent their depletion or destruction. With increasing human population and consumption, the impact on biodiversity and natural habitats has become significant. Conservation efforts aim to mitigate these impacts and ensure the long-term sustainability of life on Earth.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the effects of human activities on the environment, limited to: habitat destruction, e.g. deforestation, drainage of swampland, damming rivers, overfishing; pollution, e.g. sewage, industrial waste, fertilisers, pesticides, plastics.
  2. Describe the consequences of these effects, limited to: extinction of species; loss of genetic variety; loss of food source; acid rain; global warming; climate change.
  3. Describe the need for conservation of: habitats; species; genetic variety.
  4. Describe methods of conservation, limited to: managing fishing; maintaining biodiversity; recycling; sustainable use of resources; education.

Human Impact on the Environment:

Human activities have profound effects on the environment, leading to various forms of degradation:

1. Habitat Destruction:

Destruction of natural habitats is a major cause of biodiversity loss. Examples include:

  • **Deforestation:** Clearing forests for agriculture, logging, or urban development. This leads to loss of habitats, soil erosion, increased flooding, and an increase in atmospheric carbon dioxide.
  • **Drainage of swampland:** Converting wetlands into agricultural or urban areas, destroying unique ecosystems.
  • **Damming rivers:** Alters natural water flow, impacts aquatic ecosystems, and can displace communities.
  • **Overfishing:** Depleting fish stocks faster than they can replenish, leading to species extinction and loss of food sources.

*Figure: An area affected by deforestation, illustrating habitat destruction.*

2. Pollution:

Pollution introduces harmful substances into the environment:

  • **Sewage:** Untreated sewage in water bodies leads to oxygen depletion and spread of disease.
  • **Industrial waste:** Can contain toxic chemicals that harm ecosystems and human health.
  • **Fertilisers:** Excess fertilizers leaching into water bodies cause eutrophication (algal blooms, oxygen depletion, death of aquatic life).
  • **Pesticides:** Chemicals that can accumulate in food chains (bioaccumulation) and reduce biodiversity by killing non-target species.
  • **Plastics:** Persistent pollutants that harm wildlife through entanglement and ingestion, and break down into microplastics.

*Figure: Plastic waste polluting an ocean environment.*

Consequences of Environmental Impacts:

The consequences of human activities on the environment are far-reaching:

  • **Extinction of species:** Habitat loss and pollution drive many species to extinction.
  • **Loss of genetic variety:** Reduced biodiversity means less genetic variation within species, making populations more vulnerable to disease or environmental change.
  • **Loss of food source:** Overfishing and habitat destruction deplete food sources for both humans and other organisms.
  • **Acid rain:** Caused by sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides, damaging forests, lakes, and buildings.
  • **Global warming and climate change:** Caused by increased greenhouse gas emissions, leading to rising temperatures, extreme weather, and sea-level rise.

The Need for Conservation:

**Conservation** is essential to:

  • **Protect habitats:** Preserve ecosystems that provide essential services (e.g., clean water, air, pollination).
  • **Conserve species:** Prevent extinction and maintain biodiversity.
  • **Maintain genetic variety:** Ensure populations have the genetic diversity to adapt to future changes.
  • **Ensure sustainable use of resources:** Manage natural resources so they can be used by present and future generations.

Methods of Conservation:

Various methods are employed for conservation:

  • **Managing fishing:** Implementing quotas, fishing seasons, and sustainable fishing practices to prevent overfishing.
  • **Maintaining biodiversity:** Protecting endangered species, establishing national parks and protected areas, seed banks.
  • **Recycling:** Reducing waste and conserving raw materials by reusing materials (e.g., plastics, metals, paper).
  • **Sustainable use of resources:** Using resources at a rate that allows them to replenish naturally, minimizing long-term depletion.
  • **Education:** Raising public awareness about environmental issues and the importance of conservation.
