P6.4 Galaxies and the Universe

Our Solar System is just a tiny part of a much larger structure called a galaxy, and our galaxy is one among billions in the vastness of the Universe. This section explores the scale of the Universe, the nature of galaxies, and the prevailing scientific theory about the Universe's origin and evolution: the Big Bang Theory.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Know that: galaxies are each made up of many billions of stars, the Sun is a star in the galaxy known as the Milky Way, other stars that make up the Milky Way are much further away from the Earth than the Sun is from the Earth.
  2. Know that the Milky Way is one of many billions of galaxies making up the Universe and that the diameter of the Milky Way is approximately 100 000 light-years.
  3. Know that the Big Bang Theory is supported by many astronomical observations and states that: the Universe expanded from a single point of high density and temperature, the Universe is still expanding, the Universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old.

Galaxies: Cosmic Islands of Stars:

A **galaxy** is a massive system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter, all bound together by gravity. Each galaxy is made up of many billions of stars. Our Sun is just one of these stars, located within the spiral galaxy known as the **Milky Way**.

While the Sun is our closest star, other stars within the Milky Way are vastly further away from Earth than the Sun is. This is why they appear as tiny points of light in the night sky, even though many are much larger and brighter than our Sun. The Milky Way itself is an enormous structure, with an approximate diameter of **100,000 light-years**.

*Figure: An artist's impression of the Milky Way galaxy, showing its spiral arms and central bulge.*

The Expanding Universe and the Big Bang Theory:

The **Universe** is made up of many billions of galaxies, and it is continuously expanding. The prevailing scientific model describing the origin and evolution of our Universe is the **Big Bang Theory**. This theory is supported by a wealth of astronomical observations, including the redshift of distant galaxies and the existence of the cosmic microwave background radiation.

The Big Bang Theory states that:

  • The Universe expanded from a single point of extremely high density and temperature.
  • The Universe is still expanding today.
  • The Universe is approximately **13.8 billion years old**.

The observation that distant galaxies are moving away from us, and that more distant galaxies are moving away faster, is known as **redshift**. This phenomenon, where light from receding objects is stretched to longer (redder) wavelengths, provides strong evidence for the ongoing expansion of the Universe, a key prediction of the Big Bang Theory.
