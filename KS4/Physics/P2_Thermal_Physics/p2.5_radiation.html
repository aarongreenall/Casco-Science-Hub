P2.5 Radiation

Thermal radiation is a method of heat transfer that does not require a medium, making it distinct from conduction and convection. It involves the emission, absorption, and reflection of electromagnetic waves, primarily infrared radiation. Understanding thermal radiation is essential for explaining how the Sun heats the Earth, how objects cool down, and the design of various everyday technologies.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Know that thermal energy transfer by thermal radiation does not require a medium and is mainly due to infrared radiation.
  2. Describe the effect of surface colour (black or white) and texture (dull or shiny) on the emission, absorption and reflection of thermal radiation.
  3. Know that the temperature of the Earth is affected by the radiation absorbed by the Earth and radiation emitted by the Earth.
  4. Describe experiments to distinguish between good and bad emitters of thermal radiation.
  5. Describe experiments to distinguish between good and bad absorbers of thermal radiation.

Understanding Thermal Radiation:

**Thermal radiation** is the transfer of thermal energy through electromagnetic waves. Unlike conduction and convection, which rely on particles, radiation can travel through a vacuum, which is how the Sun's heat reaches Earth. The primary form of electromagnetic radiation involved in thermal transfer is **infrared radiation**.

All objects with a temperature above absolute zero emit thermal radiation. The hotter an object, the more thermal radiation it emits.

Surface Properties and Radiation:

The color and texture of a surface significantly affect how well it emits, absorbs, and reflects thermal radiation:

  • **Dull, black surfaces:** Are good emitters and good absorbers of thermal radiation. They absorb most of the radiation that falls on them and emit a lot of radiation.
  • **Shiny, white surfaces:** Are poor emitters and poor absorbers of thermal radiation. They reflect most of the radiation that falls on them and emit very little.

This principle is applied in everyday life; for example, hot water tanks are often painted white and shiny to reduce heat loss by radiation, while solar panels are black and dull to maximize heat absorption.

Earth's Temperature and Radiation Balance:

The temperature of the Earth is a delicate balance between the radiation absorbed from the Sun and the radiation emitted back into space by the Earth. Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere play a crucial role by absorbing and re-emitting some of the infrared radiation from Earth's surface, trapping heat and contributing to the planet's overall temperature.
