9.3.1 Waveforms and Sound Properties

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will introduce how sound can be represented as a waveform and how the properties of the waveform relate to the properties of the sound.

Visualizing Sound:

  • Sound waves are longitudinal waves, but they can be represented graphically as a transverse wave on an oscilloscope.
  • The graph shows the displacement of particles over time.

Properties of Sound and Waveforms:

  • Pitch:
    • How high or low a sound is.
    • Determined by the frequency of the wave.
    • Frequency: The number of complete waves passing a point per second, measured in Hertz (Hz).
    • On a waveform diagram: A high frequency (high pitch) sound has waves that are close together. A low frequency (low pitch) sound has waves that are spread out.
  • Loudness:
    • The intensity of a sound.
    • Determined by the amplitude of the wave.
    • Amplitude: The maximum displacement or distance moved by a point on a vibrating body or wave measured from its equilibrium position.
    • On a waveform diagram: A large amplitude represents a loud sound. A small amplitude represents a quiet sound.

Diagrams comparing waveforms for: a) loud vs. quiet sounds (different amplitudes, same frequency), and b) high-pitch vs. low-pitch sounds (different frequencies, same amplitude) are essential here.
