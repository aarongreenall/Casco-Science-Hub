7.9.5 Circuit diagrams and symbols

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Recognise the standard symbols for common circuit components.
  2. Understand that a circuit diagram is a universal way to represent a circuit.
  3. Draw a simple series circuit diagram using the correct symbols.

The Language of Circuits

Drawing a realistic picture of an electrical circuit every time would be very slow and complicated. Instead, scientists and electricians use a universal "language" of symbols to draw circuits. A drawing that uses these standard symbols is called a **circuit diagram**.

Analogy: The Map of a Circuit

A circuit diagram is like a map. The **symbols** are the map icons (like a symbol for a hospital or a train station), and the **lines** are the **roads** that connect everything together. Using a standard map with standard symbols means that anyone, anywhere in the world, can understand how to get from one place to another. In the same way, a standard circuit diagram allows any scientist or electrician to build the exact same circuit, even if they don't speak the same language.

Common Circuit Symbols

Here are some of the most common symbols you will need to know.

Component Symbol Function
CellProvides the "push" (voltage) to the circuit.
BatteryA collection of two or more cells.
Switch (Open)Breaks the circuit and stops the current.
Switch (Closed)Completes the circuit and allows current to flow.
Bulb (Lamp)Lights up when current flows through it.
AmmeterMeasures the current (in Amps). Connected in series.
VoltmeterMeasures the voltage (in Volts). Connected in parallel.
ResistorOpposes the flow of current.

In-School Activity: Build and Draw

This activity has two parts to test your skills.

Part 1: Diagram to Circuit

Your teacher will give you a circuit diagram for a simple series circuit. Your task is to use the real components (battery, wires, bulbs, switch) to build the circuit exactly as shown in the diagram.

Part 2: Circuit to Diagram

Your teacher will show you a pre-built circuit. Your task is to draw the correct circuit diagram for it in your notebook, using the standard symbols.

Interactive Simulation: Circuit Construction Kit

This PhET simulation is the perfect tool for practicing with circuit diagrams. As you drag and drop the realistic-looking components, the simulation automatically shows you their correct scientific symbols in the diagram view.
