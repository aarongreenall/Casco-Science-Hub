C10.1 Water

Water is an essential compound for all known forms of life and plays a crucial role in the environment. Ensuring a clean and safe domestic water supply is vital for public health. This section explores the processes involved in treating water to make it potable (safe for drinking).

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the treatment of the domestic water supply in terms of: sedimentation and filtration to remove solids; use of carbon to remove tastes and odours; chlorination to kill microbes.

Stages of Domestic Water Treatment:

The process of treating water to make it safe for domestic consumption typically involves several stages to remove impurities and harmful substances:

1. Sedimentation:

Raw water from rivers, lakes, or reservoirs often contains suspended solid particles (like sand, silt, and clay). In the **sedimentation** stage, the water is allowed to stand still in large tanks. Heavier particles settle to the bottom due to gravity. Sometimes, a coagulant (like aluminum sulfate) is added to make smaller particles clump together, forming larger flocs that settle more easily.

*Figure: A sedimentation tank in a water treatment plant, where heavier particles settle out.*

2. Filtration:

After sedimentation, the water passes through **filtration** beds, typically made of layers of sand and gravel. This process removes any remaining suspended solids, as well as some microorganisms, that did not settle during sedimentation.

*Figure: Diagram of a sand filter, showing layers of sand and gravel for water purification.*

3. Carbon Treatment (Activated Carbon):

After filtration, water may still contain dissolved organic impurities that cause unpleasant tastes and odours. These are removed by passing the water through beds of **activated carbon** (charcoal). Activated carbon has a very porous structure and a large surface area, allowing it to adsorb (trap on its surface) these impurities.

4. Chlorination:

The final stage of water treatment is **chlorination**, where a small amount of chlorine gas (or chlorine compounds) is added to the water. Chlorine is a powerful disinfectant that effectively **kills microbes** (bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms) that could cause disease. This ensures the water is safe for consumption.

*Figure: Chlorine gas, used as a disinfectant in water treatment.*
