B9.3 Blood vessels

The circulatory system relies on a complex network of **blood vessels** to transport blood throughout the body. These vessels—arteries, veins, and capillaries—each have specialized structures adapted to their specific functions in carrying blood away from the heart, back to the heart, or facilitating exchange with tissues.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe the structure and functions of arteries, veins and capillaries.
  2. State the advantages of a closed circulatory system.
  3. Name the main blood vessels entering and leaving the heart, lungs and kidneys, limited to: vena cava, pulmonary artery, pulmonary vein, aorta, renal artery, renal vein.
  4. Describe the advantages of a double circulatory system.
  5. Describe the route of the blood in the circulatory system, limited to: heart $\rightarrow$ lungs $\rightarrow$ heart $\rightarrow$ body.

Structure and Functions of Blood Vessels:

The three main types of blood vessels are arteries, veins, and capillaries, each adapted for its role in blood transport:

*Figure: Comparison of the structure of an artery, capillary, and vein.*

  • **Arteries:** * **Function:** Carry blood **away from the heart** to the rest of the body. * **Structure:** Have thick, muscular, elastic walls to withstand high pressure from heartbeats. A narrow lumen (internal space).
  • **Veins:** * **Function:** Carry blood **back to the heart** from the body. * **Structure:** Have thinner, less muscular walls than arteries, as blood pressure is lower. A wider lumen. Contain **valves** to prevent backflow of blood, especially against gravity.
  • **Capillaries:** * **Function:** Form a vast network connecting arteries and veins, facilitating the **exchange of substances** (oxygen, nutrients, waste products) between blood and body tissues. * **Structure:** Extremely narrow lumen (only wide enough for one red blood cell at a time). Walls are **one cell thick**, providing a very short diffusion distance for efficient exchange.

Advantages of a Closed and Double Circulatory System:

As previously discussed, mammals have a **closed, double circulatory system**, which offers significant advantages:

  • **Closed System Advantage:** Blood is always contained within vessels, allowing for higher blood pressure and more efficient transport to all tissues.
  • **Double System Advantage:** Blood passes through the heart twice per circuit. This allows for: * **Higher pressure to body tissues:** Blood returning from the lungs is re-pumped by the heart to the body, ensuring faster delivery of oxygen and nutrients. * **Efficient gas exchange:** Separate circuits for lungs and body prevent mixing of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood, maximizing oxygen delivery.

Route of Blood in the Circulatory System and Major Vessels:

Blood follows a specific route through the circulatory system:

Heart $\rightarrow$ Lungs (Pulmonary Artery) $\rightarrow$ Heart (Pulmonary Vein) $\rightarrow$ Body (Aorta) $\rightarrow$ Heart (Vena Cava)

Key blood vessels involved:

  • **Vena Cava:** Brings deoxygenated blood from the body to the right atrium of the heart.
  • **Pulmonary Artery:** Carries deoxygenated blood from the right ventricle of the heart to the lungs. (Note: It's an artery because it carries blood *away* from the heart).
  • **Pulmonary Vein:** Brings oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atrium of the heart. (Note: It's a vein because it carries blood *towards* the heart).
  • **Aorta:** Carries oxygenated blood from the left ventricle of the heart to the rest of the body.
  • **Renal Artery:** Carries oxygenated blood from the aorta to the kidneys.
  • **Renal Vein:** Carries deoxygenated blood from the kidneys back to the vena cava.
