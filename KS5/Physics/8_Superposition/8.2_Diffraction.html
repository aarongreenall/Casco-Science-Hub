8.2 Diffraction

Learning Objectives:

  1. Explain the meaning of the term diffraction.
  2. Show an understanding of experiments that demonstrate diffraction including the qualitative effect of the gap width relative to the wavelength of the wave.

LO1: The Meaning of Diffraction

**Diffraction** is the phenomenon where waves spread out as they pass through an aperture (a gap or slit) or as they pass around an obstacle. It is a characteristic property of all types of waves, including sound, light, and water waves.

This spreading out is a direct consequence of Huygens' principle, which states that every point on a wavefront can be considered as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. When a wavefront passes through a gap, only the points within the gap can act as sources. The superposition of these secondary wavelets causes the wave to spread into the region that would otherwise be a geometric shadow.

A real-world example is being able to hear someone talking from around a corner. The sound waves diffract around the edge of the corner, allowing them to travel to your ear even though there is no direct line of sight to the person speaking.

LO2: Demonstrating Diffraction and the Effect of Gap Width

The extent to which a wave diffracts depends critically on the relationship between the wavelength of the wave ($\lambda$) and the size of the gap or obstacle.

The most significant diffraction occurs when the wavelength of the wave is approximately equal to the size of the gap.

When gap width $>> \lambda$, diffraction is unnoticeable.

When gap width $\approx \lambda$, diffraction is most significant.

When gap width $< \lambda$, the wave spreads out significantly, but most of the energy is reflected.

Demonstration with a Ripple Tank

A ripple tank is an excellent tool for demonstrating diffraction. A vibrating bar creates plane water waves of a specific wavelength. These waves are directed towards a barrier with a gap of adjustable width. By changing the gap width and the wave frequency (which changes the wavelength), the relationship between them can be clearly observed.
