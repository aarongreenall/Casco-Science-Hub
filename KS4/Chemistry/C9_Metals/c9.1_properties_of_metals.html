C9.1 Properties of metals

Metals and non-metals represent two broad categories of elements with distinct general physical properties. Understanding these differences is crucial for predicting their behavior and applications in various fields, from construction to electronics.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Compare the general physical properties of metals and non-metals, including: thermal conductivity, electrical conductivity, malleability and ductility, melting and boiling points.

General Physical Properties:

Here's a comparison of the general physical properties of metals and non-metals:

1. Thermal Conductivity:

  • **Metals:** Generally good thermal conductors. They transfer heat efficiently (e.g., metal cooking pans).
  • **Non-metals:** Generally poor thermal conductors (thermal insulators). (Diamond is a notable exception, being an excellent thermal conductor).

2. Electrical Conductivity:

  • **Metals:** Generally good electrical conductors. They allow electric current to flow easily due to delocalised electrons (e.g., copper in electrical wires).
  • **Non-metals:** Generally poor electrical conductors (electrical insulators). (Graphite is a notable exception, being a good electrical conductor).

3. Malleability and Ductility:

  • **Metals:** Generally **malleable** (can be hammered into sheets) and **ductile** (can be drawn into wires). This is due to the layers of positive ions being able to slide over each other in the 'sea' of delocalised electrons without breaking the metallic bond.
  • **Non-metals:** Generally brittle (if solid) or exist as gases/liquids. Solid non-metals are not malleable or ductile.

*Figure: Copper being hammered into shape, demonstrating its malleability.*

4. Melting and Boiling Points:

  • **Metals:** Generally have high melting points and boiling points due to strong metallic bonds.
  • **Non-metals:** Generally have low melting points and boiling points (many are gases or liquids at room temperature) due to weak intermolecular forces between simple molecules.
