B12.1 Respiration

**Respiration** is a fundamental metabolic process that occurs in all living cells to release energy from nutrient molecules. This energy is essential for all life processes, including growth, movement, and maintaining body temperature. Respiration can occur in two main forms: aerobic (with oxygen) and anaerobic (without oxygen), each producing different amounts of energy and different byproducts.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Describe respiration as the chemical reactions in cells that break down nutrient molecules and release energy for metabolism.
  2. State that the energy released from respiration is used for: muscle contraction; protein synthesis; cell division; active transport; maintenance of a constant body temperature.
  3. State the word equation for aerobic respiration as: glucose + oxygen $\rightarrow$ carbon dioxide + water.
  4. State the balanced symbol equation for aerobic respiration as: $C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O$.
  5. State the word equation for anaerobic respiration in humans as: glucose $\rightarrow$ lactic acid.
  6. State the word equation for anaerobic respiration in yeast as: glucose $\rightarrow$ ethanol + carbon dioxide.
  7. Describe the differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration in terms of: oxygen requirements; relative amounts of energy released; products.
  8. Describe the uses of anaerobic respiration in the production of ethanol by yeast (fermentation) and in muscle cells during vigorous exercise.
  9. Explain the concept of oxygen debt.

What is Respiration?

**Respiration** is a series of chemical reactions that occur inside living cells to break down nutrient molecules (primarily glucose) and **release energy** for metabolism. This energy is stored in a molecule called ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which powers various cellular activities.

The energy released from respiration is used for essential life processes, including:

  • **Muscle contraction:** For movement.
  • **Protein synthesis:** Building new proteins for growth and repair.
  • **Cell division:** Producing new cells for growth and repair.
  • **Active transport:** Moving substances against a concentration gradient across cell membranes.
  • **Maintenance of a constant body temperature:** In mammals and birds (endotherms).

Aerobic Respiration:

**Aerobic respiration** occurs in the presence of **oxygen**. It is the most efficient form of respiration, releasing a large amount of energy from each glucose molecule. It takes place mainly in the mitochondria of cells.

**Word equation:**

`Glucose + Oxygen -> Carbon dioxide + Water`

**Balanced symbol equation:**

$C_6H_{12}O_6(aq) + 6O_2(g) \rightarrow 6CO_2(g) + 6H_2O(l)$

*Figure: A simplified diagram illustrating the inputs and outputs of aerobic respiration.*

Anaerobic Respiration:

**Anaerobic respiration** occurs in the **absence of oxygen**. It is much less efficient than aerobic respiration, releasing a smaller amount of energy from each glucose molecule.

**In humans (during vigorous exercise):** When muscles do not receive enough oxygen for aerobic respiration, they switch to anaerobic respiration.

`Glucose -> Lactic acid`

Lactic acid buildup causes muscle fatigue and pain. After exercise, oxygen is needed to break down the accumulated lactic acid; this extra oxygen is called the **oxygen debt**.

**In yeast (fermentation):** Yeast carries out anaerobic respiration to produce ethanol and carbon dioxide. This process is known as **fermentation** and is used in brewing (alcohol production) and baking (carbon dioxide makes bread rise).

`Glucose -> Ethanol + Carbon dioxide`

*Figure: A simplified diagram illustrating the inputs and outputs of anaerobic respiration in humans and yeast.*

Differences between Aerobic and Anaerobic Respiration:

  • **Oxygen Requirements:** * Aerobic: Requires oxygen. * Anaerobic: Does not require oxygen.
  • **Relative Amounts of Energy Released:** * Aerobic: Releases a large amount of energy. * Anaerobic: Releases a small amount of energy.
  • **Products:** * Aerobic: Carbon dioxide and water. * Anaerobic (humans): Lactic acid. * Anaerobic (yeast): Ethanol and carbon dioxide.
