8.7.2 Effects of Forces on Motion

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will describe how forces can change the motion of an object.

What is a Force?

  • A force is a push or a pull.
  • Forces are measured in Newtons (N).
  • Forces can change an object's speed, direction of motion, or shape.

Balanced and Unbalanced Forces:

  • Forces acting on an object can be balanced or unbalanced.
  • Balanced Forces: When the forces acting on an object are equal in size and opposite in direction.
    • Effect: There is no change in motion. An object at rest stays at rest, and a moving object continues to move at a constant speed in a straight line.
    • The resultant force is zero.
  • Unbalanced Forces: When the forces acting on an object are not equal and opposite.
    • Effect: There is a change in motion. The object will accelerate (speed up), decelerate (slow down), or change direction.
    • There is a non-zero resultant force.

Types of Forces:

  • Friction: A force that opposes motion between surfaces in contact.
  • Air Resistance (Drag): A type of friction that acts on objects moving through the air.
  • Gravity (Weight): The force of attraction between objects with mass.
  • Upthrust: An upward force exerted by a fluid that opposes the weight of an immersed object.

Examples: A car moving at a constant speed has balanced forces (driving force equals air resistance and friction). A car accelerating has unbalanced forces (driving force is greater than resistive forces).
