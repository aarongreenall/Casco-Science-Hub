C6.1 Physical and chemical changes

In chemistry, it is essential to distinguish between **physical changes** and **chemical changes**. While both involve alterations to matter, they differ fundamentally in whether new substances are formed and whether the change is easily reversible. Understanding this distinction is key to comprehending chemical reactions and the properties of matter.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Identify physical and chemical changes, and understand the differences between them.

Physical Changes:

A **physical change** is a change in the form or appearance of a substance, but not its chemical composition. No new substances are formed during a physical change. These changes are often easily reversible.

Characteristics of physical changes:

  • The chemical identity of the substance remains the same.
  • No new chemical bonds are formed or broken.
  • Often reversible (e.g., melting ice can be refrozen).
  • Energy changes are usually relatively small.

Examples of physical changes include:

  • **Changes of state:** Melting ice (solid water to liquid water), boiling water (liquid water to gaseous water/steam).
  • **Dissolving:** Sugar dissolving in water.
  • **Crushing or breaking:** Crushing a can, breaking glass.
  • **Mixing:** Mixing sand and water.

*Figure: Melting ice is a physical change; water remains water, just in a different state.*

Chemical Changes (Chemical Reactions):

A **chemical change** (or **chemical reaction**) is a process that involves the rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance, resulting in the formation of **new substances** with different chemical properties. These changes are often difficult to reverse.

Characteristics of chemical changes:

  • New substances are formed.
  • Chemical bonds are broken and/or formed.
  • Often difficult or impossible to reverse by physical means.
  • Often accompanied by significant energy changes (heat, light, sound).
  • Indicators of a chemical change: formation of a gas (bubbles), formation of a precipitate (solid), color change, temperature change, emission of light or sound.

Examples of chemical changes include:

  • **Burning:** Wood burning to ash and smoke.
  • **Rusting:** Iron reacting with oxygen and water to form iron oxide (rust).
  • **Digestion:** Food breaking down in your body.
  • **Baking a cake:** Ingredients react to form a new substance.

*Figure: Rusting is a chemical change where iron reacts with oxygen and water to form a new substance, iron oxide.*
