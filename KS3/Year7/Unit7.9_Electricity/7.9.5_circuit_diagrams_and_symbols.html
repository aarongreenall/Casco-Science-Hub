7.9.5 Circuit Diagrams and Symbols

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will introduce the standard symbols used to draw circuit diagrams.

Why use diagrams? Circuit diagrams are a universal way for scientists and engineers to represent circuits clearly and simply, avoiding the confusion of drawing realistic pictures of components.

Common Circuit Symbols:

Component Symbol Function
Wire ──── Allows current to flow.
Cell ─|│─ Provides voltage. The long line is the positive (+) terminal.
Battery ─|│|│─ A combination of two or more cells.
Switch (Open) ──/ ── Breaks the circuit, stopping current flow.
Switch (Closed) ───── Completes the circuit, allowing current to flow.
Bulb / Lamp ─(X)─ Converts electrical energy to light and heat.
Ammeter ─(A)─ Measures current. Connected in series.
Voltmeter ─(V)─ Measures voltage. Connected in parallel.
Resistor ─[ ]─ Resists the flow of current.

Practice drawing simple series circuits using these symbols.
