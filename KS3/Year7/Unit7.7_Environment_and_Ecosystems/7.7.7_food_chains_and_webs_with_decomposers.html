7.7.7 Food Chains and Webs with Decomposers

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain food chains and food webs, emphasizing the role of decomposers in these systems.

Key concepts:

  • Food Chain: Shows how energy is transferred from one living organism to another through feeding. Starts with a producer and arrows point in the direction of energy flow.
    • Example: Grass → Rabbit → Fox
  • Producer: An organism that makes its own food, usually through photosynthesis (e.g., plants, algae).
  • Consumer: An organism that gets energy by eating other organisms.
    • Primary consumer (herbivore): Eats producers.
    • Secondary consumer (carnivore/omnivore): Eats primary consumers.
    • Tertiary consumer: Eats secondary consumers.
  • Food Web: A network of interconnected food chains, showing more complex feeding relationships in an ecosystem.
  • Role of Decomposers in Food Webs: Decomposers break down dead producers and consumers at all trophic levels, returning nutrients to the ecosystem. They are a crucial link that is often not explicitly drawn in every food chain diagram but is always present.
  • Importance of biodiversity for stable food webs.
