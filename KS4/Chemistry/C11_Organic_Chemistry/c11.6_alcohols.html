C11.6 Alcohols

**Alcohols** are a homologous series of organic compounds characterized by the presence of a hydroxyl functional group (-OH) bonded to a carbon atom. They are versatile compounds with various uses as fuels and solvents. This section focuses on ethanol, its properties, and its complete combustion.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Name and draw the displayed formulas of: ethanol.
  2. State the uses of ethanol as: a solvent; a fuel.
  3. Describe the complete combustion of ethanol.

Ethanol: Structure and Properties:

**Ethanol (C$_2$H$_5$OH)** is the simplest alcohol you need to know. It contains two carbon atoms, five hydrogen atoms, and one hydroxyl (-OH) group. The -OH group is responsible for many of alcohol's characteristic chemical properties.

You should be able to draw the **displayed formula** of ethanol, showing all atoms and all bonds.

*Figure: The displayed formula of ethanol, showing the hydroxyl (-OH) functional group.*

Uses of Ethanol:

Ethanol has several important uses due to its properties:

  • **As a Solvent:** Ethanol is an excellent solvent, capable of dissolving many organic substances that do not dissolve in water. It is used in cosmetics (perfumes, aftershaves), food flavourings and colourings, medicinal preparations (e.g., tinctures), and as an industrial solvent.
  • **As a Fuel:** Ethanol is a flammable liquid that burns readily, releasing a significant amount of energy. It is used as a fuel in some vehicles (e.g., in blends with petrol) and in spirit burners.

Complete Combustion of Ethanol:

Like other hydrocarbons and organic compounds, ethanol undergoes **complete combustion** when burned in a plentiful supply of oxygen. The products of complete combustion are always **carbon dioxide (CO$_2$)** and **water (H$_2$O)**.

The balanced chemical equation for the complete combustion of ethanol is:

`C2H5OH(l) + 3O2(g) -> 2CO2(g) + 3H2O(l)`

During combustion, chemical energy stored in the bonds of ethanol is converted into thermal energy (heat) and light energy.
