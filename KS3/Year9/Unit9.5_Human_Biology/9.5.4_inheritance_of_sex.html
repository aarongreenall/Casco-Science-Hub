9.5.4 Inheritance of Sex

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Describe the inheritance of sex in humans in terms of XX and XY chromosomes.

The Chromosomes That Decide

As we've learned, humans have 46 chromosomes in their body cells, arranged in 23 pairs. The first 22 pairs are numbered and are the same in both males and females. However, the 23rd pair is special: these are the **sex chromosomes**, and they determine an individual's biological sex.

Females: XX

Females have two **X chromosomes**. Their 23rd pair is written as **XX**.

Males: XY

Males have one **X chromosome** and one **Y chromosome**. Their 23rd pair is written as **XY**.

Passing on the Chromosomes

Remember that gametes (sperm and eggs) only have half the number of chromosomesâ€”23 in total. This means they only have **one** sex chromosome.

  • Since females are **XX**, all of their egg cells will contain a single **X chromosome**.
  • Since males are **XY**, half of their sperm cells will contain an **X chromosome**, and the other half will contain a **Y chromosome**.

This means that the sex of a baby is determined by which type of sperm fertilises the egg. If a sperm with an X chromosome fertilises the egg, the resulting zygote will be XX (a girl). If a sperm with a Y chromosome fertilises the egg, the zygote will be XY (a boy).

Analogy: A Coin Toss

Think of it like this: the mother's egg always contributes an X chromosome (like always getting "Heads"). The father's sperm is like flipping a coin. There's a 50% chance it will be an X chromosome ("Heads") and a 50% chance it will be a Y chromosome ("Tails"). The outcome of this "coin toss" at fertilisation determines the baby's sex.

Predicting the Outcome: The Punnett Square

We can use a simple genetic diagram called a **Punnett square** to show the possible combinations of sex chromosomes and predict the probability of having a boy or a girl.

Mother's Gametes (Eggs)
X X
Father's Gametes (Sperm) X XX XX
Y XY XY

The Punnett square shows that for any pregnancy, there are four possible outcomes:

  • 2 chances of producing a female (XX)
  • 2 chances of producing a male (XY)

This means there is always a 50% probability of having a girl and a 50% probability of having a boy.

At-Home Experiment: Family Trait Survey

Just like biological sex, many other traits are inherited on your chromosomes. You can investigate the inheritance of traits in your own family!

Task:

Survey as many of your biological family members as you can (parents, siblings, grandparents) for the simple inherited traits below. Record your results in a table.

  • Tongue Rolling: Can they roll their tongue into a 'U' shape? (Yes/No)
  • Earlobe Attachment: Are their earlobes attached directly to the side of their head, or do they hang free? (Attached/Detached)
  • Dimples: Do they have dimples in their cheeks when they smile? (Yes/No)

Scientific Explanation:

These traits are controlled by genes passed down on chromosomes from parents to offspring. By surveying your family, you can see how different combinations of genes from your parents lead to a variety of traits among siblings. You are a unique combination of your parents' DNA!
