P3.1 General properties of waves

Waves are ubiquitous in the physical world, serving as a fundamental mechanism for transferring energy without transferring matter. From the ripples on a pond to the light that allows us to see and the sound that enables us to hear, waves are essential to countless phenomena. This section explores the general properties common to all waves, including their features and the two main types: transverse and longitudinal.

Learning Objectives:

  1. Know that waves transfer energy without transferring matter.
  2. Describe what is meant by wave motion as illustrated by vibration (oscillation) in ropes and springs and by experiments using water waves.
  3. Describe the features of a wave in terms of wavelength, frequency, crest (peak), trough, amplitude and wave speed.
  4. Recall and use the equation for wave speed $v = f\lambda$.
  5. Know that for a transverse wave, the direction of vibration is at right angles to the direction of propagation and understand that electromagnetic radiation, water waves and seismic S-waves (secondary) are transverse.
  6. Know that for a longitudinal wave, the direction of vibration is parallel to the direction of propagation and understand that sound waves and seismic P-waves (primary) are longitudinal.

Wave Fundamentals:

A **wave** is a disturbance that transfers energy from one point to another without the net transfer of matter. Imagine a ripple expanding on a pond after a stone is dropped; the water itself doesn't move across the pond, but the energy of the disturbance does. This is the essence of wave motion.

Key features used to describe waves include:

  • **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** The distance between two consecutive corresponding points on a wave (e.g., crest to crest or trough to trough).
  • **Frequency ($f$):** The number of complete waves (or oscillations) that pass a point per second. Measured in Hertz (Hz).
  • **Crest (Peak):** The highest point of a wave.
  • **Trough:** The lowest point of a wave.
  • **Amplitude:** The maximum displacement or distance moved by a point on a vibrating body or wave measured from its equilibrium position. It is related to the energy carried by the wave.
  • **Wave speed ($v$):** The speed at which the wave travels through a medium.

These features are related by the wave equation:

$v = f \times \lambda$

Where $v$ is wave speed, $f$ is frequency, and $\lambda$ is wavelength.

Types of Waves: Transverse and Longitudinal:

Waves can be classified into two main types based on the direction of particle vibration relative to the direction of energy propagation:

  • **Transverse Waves:** The direction of particle vibration (oscillation) is **at right angles (perpendicular)** to the direction of wave propagation (energy transfer). * Examples: Electromagnetic radiation (light, radio waves, X-rays), water waves, and seismic S-waves (secondary waves).
  • **Longitudinal Waves:** The direction of particle vibration (oscillation) is **parallel** to the direction of wave propagation (energy transfer). * Examples: Sound waves and seismic P-waves (primary waves).
