7.9.3 Measuring Current in Series Circuits

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how to measure electric current and describe its behavior in a series circuit.

Key concepts:

  • Electric Current (I): The rate of flow of electric charge. Measured in Amperes (A) or Amps.
  • Ammeter: An instrument used to measure electric current.
    • It must be connected in series in the circuit, meaning the current flows through the ammeter.
    • Circuit symbol for an ammeter.
  • Series Circuit: A circuit where components are connected one after another, forming a single path for the current to flow.
    • If one component breaks (e.g., a bulb blows), the entire circuit is broken, and all components stop working.
    • The current is the same at all points in a series circuit. If you place an ammeter at different points in a series circuit, it will show the same reading.
  • Simple circuit diagrams showing how to connect an ammeter in series.
