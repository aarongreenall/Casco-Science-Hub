7.1.5 Levels of organization

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Describe the arrangement of cells into tissues, organs, and organ systems.
  2. Explain the meaning of each level of organisation.
  3. Give examples of each level of organisation in an animal.

From Cells to Organisms

In complex, multicellular organisms like you, cells don't just float around randomly. They are organised into a clear hierarchy, like a well-structured company or an army. Each level of organisation is built upon the one below it, from the simplest cell to the entire organism.

Analogy: The LEGO Castle

Let's complete our LEGO analogy. We know a single LEGO brick is like a cell. The levels of organisation are like building a giant castle from those bricks:

  • A **Cell** is one single **LEGO brick**.
  • A **Tissue** is a group of similar bricks put together to make a simple **wall**.
  • An **Organ** is a structure made of different walls and parts, like a **castle tower**.
  • An **Organ System** is a group of towers and walls working together, like the castle's entire **defence system**.
  • An **Organism** is the finished **LEGO Castle**, with all its systems working together.
