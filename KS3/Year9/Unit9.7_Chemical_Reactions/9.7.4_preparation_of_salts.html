9.7.4 Preparation of Salts

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will outline the common laboratory methods for preparing soluble and insoluble salts.

What is a Salt?

A salt is an ionic compound formed when the hydrogen in an acid is replaced by a metal or ammonium ion.

1. Preparing Soluble Salts (from an insoluble base/carbonate):

This method is used when the starting base or carbonate does not dissolve in water (e.g., copper oxide, calcium carbonate).

  1. Add the insoluble base or carbonate to a warm acid until no more reacts (the excess solid sinks to the bottom).
  2. Filter the mixture to remove the excess solid base/carbonate. The salt is now dissolved in the solution (filtrate).
  3. Gently heat the solution to evaporate some of the water and make it more concentrated.
  4. Leave the concentrated solution to cool slowly. As it cools, the salt becomes less soluble, and crystals will form.
  5. Filter the crystals from the solution, wash them with a small amount of cold distilled water, and leave them to dry.

2. Preparing Soluble Salts (by Titration):

This method is used when both the acid and the alkali are soluble (e.g., reacting hydrochloric acid with sodium hydroxide).

  1. Use a pipette to measure a known volume of alkali into a conical flask.
  2. Add a few drops of an indicator (e.g., phenolphthalein).
  3. Add acid from a burette slowly until the indicator changes color, showing the solution is neutral. Record the volume of acid added.
  4. Repeat the experiment without the indicator, adding the exact same volumes of acid and alkali.
  5. Evaporate the water from the neutral salt solution to obtain the salt crystals.

3. Preparing Insoluble Salts (by Precipitation):

This method is used to make a salt that does not dissolve in water.

  1. Mix two solutions containing the required ions. For example, to make lead(II) sulfate, you could mix a solution of lead(II) nitrate with a solution of sodium sulfate.
  2. The insoluble salt will form as a solid (precipitate).
  3. Filter the mixture to separate the solid precipitate from the solution.
  4. Wash the precipitate with distilled water to remove any impurities.
  5. Leave the precipitate to dry in a warm oven.
