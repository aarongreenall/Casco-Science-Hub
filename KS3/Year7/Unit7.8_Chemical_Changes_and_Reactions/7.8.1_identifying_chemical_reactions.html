7.8.1 Identifying chemical reactions

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Define a chemical reaction as a process that forms a new substance.
  2. Identify the common signs that a chemical reaction has occurred.
  3. Distinguish between a reversible physical change and an irreversible chemical change.

What is a Chemical Reaction?

A **chemical reaction** is a process that changes one set of chemical substances into another. The starting materials are called **reactants**, and the new substances that are formed are called **products**. The key idea is that a chemical reaction creates something new, and this change is usually difficult or impossible to reverse.

Analogy: Baking a Cake

Think about the ingredients for a cake (flour, eggs, sugar). These are your reactants. You can describe their physical properties, like colour and texture. A simple **physical change** would be mixing them together; you could still, in theory, separate them. However, when you put the mixture in the oven, the heat causes a **chemical change**. The ingredients react and transform into a completely new substance (a cake) with new properties. You can't "un-bake" the cake to get your original flour, sugar, and eggs back. The change is irreversible.

Spotting a Chemical Reaction

How can you tell if a chemical reaction has happened? You need to look for clues. Here are some of the most common signs:

Observation Conclusion
A colour changeA new substance with a different colour has been formed.
Gas is produced (fizzing/bubbling)A new substance has been formed that is a gas at room temperature.
A temperature change (gets hotter or colder)Energy is being released (exothermic) or taken in (endothermic) by the reaction.
A solid (precipitate) forms when two solutions are mixedA new substance has been formed that is insoluble in the solvent.
A smell is producedA new substance has been formed that is volatile (evaporates easily).

At-Home Experiment: Making Invisible Ink

You can see a chemical change in action by making your own secret message with invisible ink!

Equipment:
  • Half a lemon
  • A cotton bud
  • A piece of white paper
  • A heat source like a warm lightbulb or a hairdryer (ask an adult for help!)
Method:
  1. Squeeze the lemon juice into a small bowl.
  2. Dip the cotton bud into the lemon juice and write a secret message on the paper.
  3. Let the juice dry completely. The message will be invisible!
  4. To reveal the message, ask an adult to help you gently heat the paper by holding it near a warm lightbulb or using a hairdryer.
  5. The message will appear in brown! The heat causes a chemical reaction (oxidation) with the acid in the lemon juice, creating a new, coloured substance. This is an irreversible chemical change.
