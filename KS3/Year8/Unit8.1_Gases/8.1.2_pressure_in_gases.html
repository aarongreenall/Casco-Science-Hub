8.1.2 Pressure in gases

In this lesson, you will learn to:

  1. Explain that gas pressure is caused by the collisions of gas particles with the walls of a container.
  2. Describe how compressing a gas (decreasing its volume) increases its pressure.
  3. Explain why gases are compressible but liquids and solids are not.

What is Gas Pressure?

The particles in a gas are constantly moving around randomly at high speeds. As they move, they collide with each other and with the walls of their container. **Gas pressure** is the force caused by all these tiny collisions on the walls of the container.

Because there are huge spaces between the particles in a gas, it is easy to push them closer together. We say that gases are **compressible**. When you compress a gas into a smaller volume, the particles have less space to move. This means they will hit the walls of the container more often, which increases the pressure.

Analogy: A Crowded Room

Imagine a group of people wandering randomly around a large room. The people are the **gas particles**. The number of times people bump into the walls is the **pressure**. Now, imagine you keep the same number of people but make the room much smaller. The people will be more crowded and will bump into the walls much more frequently. This is exactly what happens when you compress a gas: the particles hit the walls more often, and the pressure goes up.

In-School Experiment: Compressing Air

You can feel the effects of gas pressure yourself using a simple plastic syringe.

Equipment:
  • A plastic syringe (e.g., 10 ml or 20 ml)
Method:
  1. Pull the plunger of the syringe out to draw in some air.
  2. Place your thumb firmly over the nozzle at the end to seal the air inside.
  3. Now, try to push the plunger in. What do you feel? You should feel an increasing resistance. This is the pressure of the trapped air pushing back against the plunger.
  4. Observe how far you can compress the air. You can reduce its volume significantly.
  5. Now, try the same experiment but fill the syringe with water instead of air. Can you compress the water? You will find that it is almost impossible. This is because the particles in a liquid are already packed closely together, so liquids are considered **incompressible**.
