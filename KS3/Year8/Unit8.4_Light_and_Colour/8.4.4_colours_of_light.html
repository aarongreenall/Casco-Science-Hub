8.4.4 Colours of Light

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will explain how the color of an object is determined by the light it reflects, absorbs, and transmits.

Colored Objects:

  • The color of an opaque object is determined by the colors of light it reflects. All other colors are absorbed.
    • A red book appears red because it reflects red light and absorbs all other colors.
    • A white object reflects all colors of light.
    • A black object absorbs all colors of light.
  • The color of a transparent or translucent object (a filter) is determined by the colors of light it transmits (allows to pass through).
    • A blue filter appears blue because it transmits blue light and absorbs all other colors.

Mixing Colors:

  • Additive Colour Mixing (Light):
    • Primary colors of light: Red, Green, and Blue (RGB).
    • Mixing primary colors of light creates secondary colors:
      • Red + Green = Yellow
      • Red + Blue = Magenta
      • Green + Blue = Cyan
      • Red + Green + Blue = White
    • This is used in screens (TVs, phones, computer monitors).
  • Subtractive Colour Mixing (Pigments/Paints):
    • Primary colors of pigments: Cyan, Magenta, and Yellow (CMY).
    • Mixing pigments subtracts (absorbs) colors from white light.
      • Cyan + Magenta + Yellow = Black (theoretically)
    • This is used in printing.
