8.6.3 Lifestyle and Health

Content for this section is under development. Please check back soon!

This page will discuss how various lifestyle choices can impact health, including the effects of recreational drugs.

Positive Lifestyle Choices:

  • Balanced Diet: Provides necessary nutrients for growth, repair, and energy, reducing the risk of malnutrition and obesity.
  • Regular Exercise: Strengthens the heart and muscles, improves circulation, helps maintain a healthy weight, and improves mental health.
  • Sufficient Sleep: Essential for physical and mental recovery, growth, and immune function.
  • Good Hygiene: Reduces the spread of infectious diseases.

Negative Lifestyle Choices (Recreational Drugs):

  • Drugs: Substances that alter the way the body works. Medicinal drugs are used to treat illness; recreational drugs are taken for pleasure.
  • Alcohol:
    • A depressant that slows down the nervous system, leading to slower reaction times and loss of coordination.
    • Long-term effects include liver damage (cirrhosis), heart disease, and addiction.
  • Tobacco (Nicotine):
    • Nicotine is a stimulant and is highly addictive.
    • Tar in cigarette smoke is a carcinogen (causes cancer, particularly lung cancer).
    • Carbon monoxide in smoke reduces the oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood.
    • Can lead to other conditions like bronchitis and heart disease.
  • Other recreational drugs: Can have a wide range of effects on the body and brain, often leading to addiction and serious long-term health problems.
